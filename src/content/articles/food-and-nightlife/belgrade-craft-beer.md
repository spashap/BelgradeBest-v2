## Where can I drink craft beer in Belgrade?

Belgrade is widely described as Serbia's craft-beer capital, and for a visitor the practical takeaway is that you don't have to hunt far. The microbreweries, taprooms and specialist beer pubs cluster mainly around Dorćol, Savamala and the central neighbourhoods, which makes a walking circuit feasible in a single evening. Dorćol in particular has become the shorthand for the craft scene — craft-beer guides describe several standout bars within a roughly ten-minute walk of each other — though the cluster spills across the centre and down toward the Sava. If you want to understand how these districts fit together before you set out, the [Belgrade neighbourhoods](/visit-belgrade/belgrade-neighborhoods) guide covers their character in more detail; this page stays on the beer.

To orient yourself: Dorćol sits immediately north and north-east of Republic Square, sloping toward the Danube, with its bar life concentrated along streets like Strahinjića Bana (sometimes nicknamed "Silicone Valley" for its café-and-bar crowd) and the upper Dorćol grid. Savamala lies on the right bank of the Sava just south-west of the old-town ridge, a short downhill walk from the centre. Both are walkable from Republic Square and from each other, so a craft crawl rarely needs transport.

It helps to treat craft beer as its own going-out mode rather than a sub-genre of the club scene. Taprooms and beer gardens are a calmer, daytime-friendly counterpart to the splav (river-raft) nightlife, and the field is mature enough to support guided brewery tours. If you'd rather pair a brewery visit with the wider after-dark picture, the [Belgrade nightlife](/food-and-nightlife/belgrade-nightlife) page covers bars and clubs that sit outside this article's scope.

## What are the flagship Belgrade breweries?

The most durable anchor is **Dogma Brewery**, reported as founded in 2016 in a renovated sugar-factory building near the Sava and Ada Ciganlija. Its on-site taproom and beer garden are reported to have opened in 2017. Dogma is widely cited and has several years of operation behind it, which is part of why brewery tours of the scene tend to include it. The setting — an old industrial building repurposed for brewing, with a large garden — is itself part of the appeal, and the riverside location near Ada Ciganlija makes it a natural daytime stop. Dogma is also known for releasing experimental and rotating beers (an IPA called "Hoptopod" comes up repeatedly in coverage) and for hosting events and live-music nights, so it doubles as both a brewery and a place to spend an evening.

The other domestic name to know on the brewery side is **Kabinet**, frequently described as one of Serbia's biggest craft-beer brands. You'll see Kabinet beers on tap and in bottle across the central beer bars; the brewery's own taproom is reported to sit outside the city (in the countryside on the slopes of Mount Kosmaj), so in the centre you're most likely to meet the label as a tap rather than as a destination visit. Ordering a Kabinet is a straightforward way to taste a leading Serbian craft producer without tracking down a specific venue.

Other names that come up as pioneers or producers of the scene include **Crow** and **Gvint** alongside Dogma and Kabinet — credited in craft-beer guides with helping develop Belgrade's craft scene. As with the bar segment below, treat the specific roster as illustrative and verify at the time of your visit.

## What are the best craft beer bars in Belgrade?

Beyond the breweries, the more durable central beer bar to start with is **Samo Pivo**, a long-standing and frequently-recommended spot. It is reported to occupy a converted space above a shopping passage (around Balkanska in the centre) with a large beer hall, a sizeable terrace or balcony, and 20-plus taps — a durable-leaning fixture rather than a passing arrival, so it's a reasonable place to begin if you want somewhere that has held its reputation over time. Between Dogma and Samo Pivo you have two reliable poles for an evening — one a brewery-with-garden, the other a central pub.

**Krafter** is another central, repeatedly-praised craft bar, reported on Strahinjića Bana in Dorćol. It is described as serving craft only (no commercial lagers), with a strong rotation of Serbian craft beers on draft alongside a long list of imported bottles, a warm-but-modern interior and outdoor seating — a good stop on a Dorćol crawl, though it's reported not to serve food.

Beyond these, a number of further players come up repeatedly in guides and local recommendations. Frequently-cited names include **Docker Brewery, Gunners Pub, Gvint Brewery & Taproom, Draft Bar, Brotherhood Beer House** and **Pivski Zabavnik**. Several of these have multi-year histories, but they sit in a volatile bar segment, so it's honest to treat them as examples rather than institutions. Names change, venues move, and some close — so the sensible approach is to confirm a given place is still trading before you build an evening around it.

That caution isn't meant to discourage you. The point of a craft crawl is partly discovery, and the density of the central cluster means that if one taproom has gone, another is usually within walking distance. Use the list above as a starting set of search terms rather than a fixed itinerary, and check current opening hours close to your visit.

## Can I do a craft beer tour in Belgrade?

Yes — the scene is mature enough to support themed brewery tourism, and that maturity is itself a signal of how established craft beer has become here. One reported product is an e-scooter craft-beer and brewery tour that visits multiple breweries including Dogma and Gvint, which turns the central-and-riverside spread of venues into a single guided loop. As with everything in this volatile segment, the specific tour operator and stops are indicative — confirm the offering is still running and check what it includes before booking.

If you'd rather self-guide, the Dorćol-and-central density makes a DIY crawl easy: start at a central beer bar like Samo Pivo or Krafter, work through a couple of nearby taprooms on foot, and — if you want the full brewery experience with a garden — fold in a daytime trip to Dogma near the Sava and Ada Ciganlija, which is slightly out of the central cluster but the most "destination" stop of the set.

## What domestic beer labels should I look for in Serbia?

It's worth understanding the difference between Serbia's mainstream beers and its craft labels. The popular mainstream domestic beers are **Jelen** and **Lav** — these are what you'll most often see on standard bar taps and in shops, and they are lagers rather than craft products. On the craft side, domestic labels such as **Kabinet** and **Dogma** are commonly recommended; this is indicative as of 2026, so expect the range to evolve. Ordering a Kabinet or a Dogma is a straightforward way to taste what the domestic craft sector is doing without needing to track down a specific taproom.

Knowing these names also helps you read a menu quickly: if a list leans heavily on Jelen and Lav, you're in a mainstream bar; if Kabinet, Dogma and a rotating cast of taproom names appear, you've found the craft end of the spectrum.

## How much does craft beer cost in Belgrade?

Pricing is the most perishable part of any beer guide, so treat figures as context rather than a quote. At clubs and splavovi, beer has been reported roughly 2–4 EUR, though that is indicative as of 2025–2026 and you should check current menus. For craft specifically, pours at central beer bars have been reported in the region of about 300–700 RSD (roughly 3–7 EUR) per pint — again indicative and venue-dependent. Taproom pricing varies and a craft pour will often sit at the upper end of, or above, the mainstream range — which is normal for small-batch beer anywhere. The honest advice is to read the menu rather than rely on a remembered number.

In practice, an evening on the craft scene looks less like club-going and more like a relaxed wander: a taproom or beer garden, a couple of small pours, and a short walk to the next place. The concentration in the Dorćol and central cluster is what makes this work without much planning.
