## PART C ‚Äî GROUNDING DEPTH (for KB authoring)

### C.1 The layered story (slug #1 raw material)
Chronology + what physically survives + where to see it:

- **Celtic/Roman (Singidunum)** ‚Äî Roman castrum 1st‚Äì2nd c.; 2,300-yr continuous fortification at Kalemegdan `CONFIRMED`. Most Roman fabric underground; surface-visible is fragmentary (Roman Well shaft, occasional masonry) `REPORTED`. Visitor "sees" it conceptually + via National Museum archaeology `CONFIRMED`.
- **Byzantine/medieval Serbian** ‚Äî 12th-c. Byzantine castle; seat of Despot Stefan Lazareviƒá early 15th c., capital of the Serbian Despotate `CONFIRMED`. Survives: Despot's castle + donjon (Upper Town panorama plateau), Neboj≈°a Tower (Lower Town, ticketed museum) `CONFIRMED`.
- **Ottoman (~500 yrs, fragmented)** ‚Äî Belgrade fell 1521 `REPORTED` (control was intermittent; "500 years" is the simplified narrative). Plateau = military parade ground ("fiƒáir-bajir," hill of contemplation) `CONFIRMED`. Survives: Ottoman stone ramparts (Upper Town; brick facing is later Austrian), pasha's tomb, older gates ‚Äî under-interpreted for casual visitors `REPORTED`. **Bajrakli Mosque** = only survivor of 200+ mosques `CONFIRMED` (from walking-tour sources).
- **Austrian/Habsburg + Zemun** ‚Äî Austrian fortress upgrades 1717‚Äì1739 `CONFIRMED`. Zemun under Habsburg rule 1717‚Äì1918, developed as separate frontier town `CONFIRMED`. Survives: Zemun old-town 18th‚Äì19th-c. street fabric; Gardo≈°/Millennium Tower (1896, marks 1,000 yrs Hungarian settlement) `CONFIRMED`.
- **Interwar Yugoslavia** ‚Äî Belgrade capital of new South-Slav kingdom; Zemun incorporated 1934 `CONFIRMED`. Eclectic/academic/modernist public buildings around Republic Square (National Museum bldg 1903, National Theatre) `CONFIRMED`.
- **Socialist modernism/Brutalism** ‚Äî see C.2.
- **Post-2000 / Belgrade Waterfront** ‚Äî Beograd na vodi, 2014 govt + Eagle Hills (Abu Dhabi) deal, ~USD 3.5B, ~1.8‚Äì2M m¬≤ `CONFIRMED`. ### C.2 Brutalism / socialist modernism (slug #2 raw material)

- **Genex Tower / Western City Gate** ‚Äî 35-floor twin towers, arch. Mihajlo Mitroviƒá, built 1977‚Äì80; rotating restaurant now closed `CONFIRMED`. Residential tower inhabited; commercial tower empty/inaccessible ‚Üí **view from outside only** `REPORTED`.
- **Blocks 23, 61‚Äì63, 28, 19a** ‚Äî Block 23 = most "authentic" original masterplan; 61‚Äì63 + 28 recommended in brutalism guides `REPORTED`. **Living residential areas ‚Äî stay on public paths**, no visitor policy `REPORTED`.
- **Eastern City Gate (Rudo)** ‚Äî three 28-storey residential towers, 1973‚Äì76, brutalist triangular form, ~190 apts each, ~1,500 residents by late '80s `CONFIRMED`. Fully residential, publicly accessible area, private interiors.
