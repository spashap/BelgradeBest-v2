# Sources — /expo-2027/tickets

> Grounding source for the `tickets` article ("Expo 2027 tickets — what we know so far"). Derived from the Belgrade Expo 2027 Knowledge Base §3 (Tickets and entry) + related, verified June 2026.
> Labels: **CONFIRMED** · **REPORTED** · **UNKNOWN** · **CONFLICT**. Honor labels exactly: CONFIRMED plainly, REPORTED attributed, UNKNOWN as "not yet announced," CONFLICT explained.
> **§5 / never-final, critical for this article:** ticket prices, categories, sales dates, and booking systems are NOT published. The article MUST NOT invent prices, dates, or a sales mechanism. "Tickets cost X" or "tickets go on sale on [date]" must NEVER be written as fact. This article's honesty about what is *unknown* is its whole value.

## 1. Ticket status (the one confirmed thing)

[CONFIRMED — HIGH-LEVEL ONLY] The official Expo 2027 programme page states "Tickets available soon." As of June 2026 it does not publish ticket prices, ticket categories, sales channels, or a sales start date.
(Source: Expo 2027 official site, programme page)

## 2. What is not yet announced (the substance of this article)

[UNKNOWN] No official ticket pricing — per-day, multi-day, or season pass — has been published by the organiser as of June 2026.
(Source: Expo 2027 official site, programme page)

[UNKNOWN] No official information on ticket categories or concessions (e.g. adult, child, student, senior, disability) or on bundled tickets, as of June 2026.
(Source: Expo 2027 official site, programme page)

[UNKNOWN] No official information on reservation or entry systems — time-slot reservations, daily capacity caps, online queueing or booking mechanisms for general visitors — as of June 2026.
(Source: Orientation27 Participants FAQ)

[UNKNOWN] No official sales start date or sales channel (official website, app, authorised resellers, on-site) has been announced as of June 2026.
(Source: Expo 2027 official site, programme page)

## 3. Useful surrounding context (grounded, helps a reader without inventing ticket facts)

[CONFIRMED] Expo 2027 runs 15 May to 15 August 2027 (93 days) at the Surčin site in Belgrade — the window any ticket would cover.
(Source: srbija2027.gov.rs; Expo 2027 official site)

[CONFIRMED — PROJECTION] The official Participants FAQ projects around 4.1 million visits over the 93 days, averaging about 44,652 per day with peaks up to around 89,304 — context for why time-slot or capacity systems may eventually appear. This is a projection, not a count.
(Source: Orientation27 Participants FAQ)
[§5] Treat 4.1M as a projection, never a fact; other statements range 2.6–4 million.

[CONFIRMED] The official Expo 2027 website is the primary channel for ticket information when published; the programme page is where the "Tickets available soon" status currently appears.
(Source: Expo 2027 official site)

[REPORTED] Some independent visitor-guide websites, explicitly unaffiliated with the organisers, already publish estimated trip costs (airport buses, taxis, city passes). These are not official Expo or government figures, are not ticket prices, and can change quickly.
(Source: independent visitor guides, noted in KB as unofficial)

- Everything about prices, categories, concessions, sales date, sales channel, booking/reservation systems is UNKNOWN — frame every one as "not yet announced as of June 2026."
- NEVER invent a price, a date, a discount, or a sales mechanism. If tempted to write a number, stop — there isn't one.
