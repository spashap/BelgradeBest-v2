# Sources — /expo-2027/guide

> Grounding source for the `guide` article — the Expo 2027 pillar / "complete visitor guide, what we know so far." Derived from the Belgrade Expo 2027 Knowledge Base + freshness recheck of official sources (4 June 2026).
> Every fact carries a label: **CONFIRMED** (official Expo/BIE/government docs) · **REPORTED** (political statements, media, in-progress contracts) · **UNKNOWN** (not yet announced) · **CONFLICT** (sources disagree).
> The generator may state CONFIRMED facts plainly, must attribute REPORTED facts as provisional, must frame UNKNOWN as "not yet announced," and must explain CONFLICT rather than flatten it.
> Honor labels exactly. Do not state any REPORTED date, figure, or ticket detail as settled. This is a "what we know so far" pillar — its honesty about the unknowns is the point.

## 1. Core event facts (the stable spine)

[CONFIRMED] The event is Expo 2027 Belgrade, a Specialised Expo (not a Universal/World Expo), hosted by Belgrade, Serbia.
(Source: BIE Expo 2027 Belgrade page, 2025–2026; Expo 2027 official About page)

[CONFIRMED] Expo 2027 Belgrade runs from 15 May to 15 August 2027 — 93 days, three full months.
(Source: BIE Expo 2027 Belgrade page; Expo 2027 official Programme page; Expo 2027 "Facts and Figures" article)

[CONFIRMED] It is the first Specialised Expo ever held in the Western Balkans, and the first world exposition held on the territory of the former Yugoslavia.
(Source: Expo 2027 official About page; BIE Expo 2027 Belgrade page)

[CONFIRMED] The official theme is "Play for Humanity: Sport and Music for All." Use the exact wording.
(Source: Expo 2027 official About page; BIE Expo 2027 Belgrade page)

[CONFIRMED] Serbia won the right to host Expo 2027 in a BIE vote, beating bids that included Spain (Málaga), the United States (Minnesota), Thailand, and Argentina.
(Source: Expo 2027 "Facts and Figures" article; Wikipedia "Expo 2027 (Belgrade, Serbia)" citing BIE)

[CONFIRMED] The Expo has two official mascots, Rastko and Milica, presented as the "superheroes of play," inspired by contemporary Serbian culture.
(Source: Expo 2027 official About page, accessed 4 June 2026)

## 2. Location and site (where it is, what it contains)

[CONFIRMED] The Expo site is in the municipality of Surčin, southwest of central Belgrade, near Nikola Tesla Airport and the Sava river.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participation Guide, 30 October 2024; Wikipedia citing BIE)

[CONFIRMED] The site is approximately 5 km from Nikola Tesla Airport and approximately 13.5 km from downtown Belgrade.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participants FAQ, 13 December 2024)

[CONFIRMED] The secure Expo site covers 25 hectares; the wider complex including village, services and parking covers 113 hectares.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participation Guide, 30 October 2024)

[CONFIRMED] The complex was master-planned by the Spanish firm Fenwick Iribarren Architects, whose proposal formed the basis of Serbia's winning bid. It includes a new National Stadium (capacity ~52,000), an aquatic centre, and the Expo Village, linked by a central landscaped promenade.
(Source: Wikipedia "Expo 2027" citing BIE; KB §6 site configuration)

[REPORTED] The site is organised into three main zones: International Pavilions (130+ countries), a Thematic Zone, and a Corporate & Best Practice Area with around 45 modular timber pavilions, all connected by the central promenade.
(Source: xmetr.com independent guide, May 2026 — non-official; corroborates KB §7 thematic structure)

## 3. Participating countries (current count — recheck before every update)

[CONFIRMED] As of 4 June 2026, the official Participants page states that 137 nations have formally committed to Expo 2027 Belgrade, with a note that more are coming. The number has grown over time (117 in mid-2025, 137 by early 2026) and may continue to grow.
(Source: Expo 2027 official Participants page, accessed 4 June 2026)

[CONFIRMED] This already exceeds the previous Specialised Expo, Astana 2017, which hosted 115–117 participating countries — making Serbia a record holder for a Specialised Expo by participant count.
(Source: Expo 2027 "117 Countries" news article, July 2025; Xinhua, June 2025)

[CONFIRMED] Each participating country is expected to have a National Day and the opportunity to present itself through pavilions, exhibitions, events, innovations, culture and cuisine.
(Source: Expo 2027 "Facts and Figures" article)

[UNKNOWN] The full public list of all 137 participants — including international organisations, cities and companies — is not yet completely published; the official page lists a large subset by name.
(Source: Expo 2027 official Participants page, accessed 4 June 2026)

## 4. Tickets and entry (the big honest unknown)

[CONFIRMED] As of 4 June 2026, the official Programme page states tickets will be "available soon." No ticket prices, categories, sales channels, or release date have been published.
(Source: Expo 2027 official Programme page, accessed 4 June 2026)

[UNKNOWN] Ticket prices, day/multi-day/season-pass structures, and concession categories (adult, child, student, senior, disability) are not yet announced.
(Source: Expo 2027 official Programme page, accessed 4 June 2026)

[UNKNOWN] Any reservation, time-slot, or daily-capacity system for general visitors is not yet announced.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participants FAQ, 13 December 2024)

[UNKNOWN] There is no announced Expo-specific visa or visa waiver for ordinary foreign visitors; visitors rely on Serbia's general visa policy. (An "Expo visa" exists, but only for accredited participants/staff, not general visitors.)
(Source: Agreement on Privileges and Benefits coverage, Expo 2027 news; KB §3 entry)

[REPORTED] As general context only: Serbia operates a long-standing visa-free policy for roughly 90 countries; most visitors from the EU, US and UK do not need a visa. Travellers must verify their own country's status with the Serbian Ministry of Foreign Affairs before booking. Not Expo-specific.
(Source: KB §3 visa context; xmetr.com independent guide, May 2026)

## 5. Getting there (high-level — full detail lives in /expo-2027/getting-there)

[CONFIRMED] The Participants FAQ states visitors will reach the site by multiple means: private vehicles, public buses, urban rail, and river transport. On-site parking accommodates up to 11,000 vehicles.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participants FAQ, 13 December 2024)

[CONFLICT] The new airport–city–Expo railway has two different framings across sources. Political statements (President Vučić, October 2024) targeted completion by 1 December 2026 with a ~15-minute airport-to-centre journey. More recent contractor/ministry reporting (2025–2026) describes the 18.3 km double-track electrified line (value ~€188.1 million) as progressing on schedule for an operational launch in spring 2027, with ~4.3 km of track laid by February 2026 and nine electric multiple-unit trains delivered between November 2025 and March 2026. Treat "spring 2027 / by end of 2026" as the live, provisional target — not a settled service date.
(Sources: Serbian Monitor quoting the President, 27 October 2024; EX-YU Aviation News, 9 May 2025, 21 February 2026, 27 September 2025)

[UNKNOWN] No detailed passenger timetable, fare scheme, or confirmed opening date for this railway during the Expo period has been published.
(Source: EX-YU Aviation News, 21 February 2026; KB §7 transport)

[REPORTED] Serbia is procuring a fleet of electric buses to connect the Surčin site with the airport, downtown and surrounding municipalities; after the event the buses pass to GSP Belgrade for regular service.
(Source: Sustainable Bus, 16 July 2025; Serbia Business / eKapija, 25 February 2025)

## 6. The programme and what's on site (concept-level — detail lives in /expo-2027/programme)

[CONFIRMED] The official concept describes three thematic pavilions plus a Forum: the Power of Play Pavilion (science and psychology of play), the Play for Progress Pavilion (innovation through playful exploration), the Play Together Pavilion (sport, music and collaborative play), and the Forum (talks, panels, workshops, networking).
(Source: Expo 2027 official About page)

[CONFIRMED] Thematic programmes highlighted by the official site include "Music of all for all," "Sport of all for all," and "Limitless Play," alongside city-wide "Playgrounds" public-space interventions.
(Source: Expo 2027 official About page)

[REPORTED] The Expo is described as featuring more than 8,000 events across its 93 days.
(Source: beinbelgrade.com consolidation, September 2025 — non-official)

[UNKNOWN] No full daily calendar — concerts, sports competitions, conferences, National Days schedule, opening/closing ceremonies, or headline performers — has been published as of June 2026; only high-level categories are described.
(Source: Expo 2027 official Programme page, accessed 4 June 2026)

## 7. Scale, visitors, and how to plan a visit

[CONFIRMED / PROJECTION] Expo 2027 is projected to attract more than 4 million visitors (commonly stated as "over 4 million" or "about 4.1 million"). Always frame as a projection, and note sources sometimes refer to "visits" rather than unique "visitors."
(Source: Expo 2027 official Programme page; "Facts and Figures" article; BIE page. Projection language per KB §5 editorial warning)

[REPORTED] Independent guidance suggests 2–3 days for the Expo itself, or 4–5 days to combine it with Belgrade sightseeing; National Days are framed as the most atmospheric days to visit, and opening week plus weekends as the busiest.
(Source: xmetr.com independent guide, May 2026 — non-official, presented as orientation not official policy)

## 8. Legacy (why the site outlasts the event — evergreen hook)

[REPORTED] Expo 2027 is framed by organisers as a "legacy-first" Expo: officials state that essentially every element will be reused. Plans describe the international participants' area becoming a new Belgrade Fair (able to host major events including ATP Masters 1000 tennis), and modular pavilions being dismantled and repurposed as schools, kindergartens and sports halls across Serbia.
(Source: Serbia Business, legacy coverage 2025–2026, quoting Expo architect/director)

[UNKNOWN] No legally binding, detailed schedule has been published specifying when each legacy element will be delivered after the Expo; current material reflects intentions and plans, not enforceable deadlines.
(Source: Serbia Business legacy coverage; KB §6)

## 9. Key labels to honor when writing

- **Dates (15 May–15 Aug 2027), theme, Specialised-Expo status, Surčin location, 5 km/13.5 km distances** are CONFIRMED — state plainly.
- **The 137-country count** is CONFIRMED-current but explicitly growing — state as "137 as of mid-2026, and rising," never as a final total. This is a recheck-before-update fact.
- **The 4-million visitor figure** is a PROJECTION — frame as expected/projected, and watch "visitors" vs "visits."
- **The railway** is the article's main CONFLICT — present both the political "by end of 2026 / 1 Dec 2026" target and the contractor "spring 2027" operational framing; do not flatten to a single settled date.
- **Tickets, prices, reservation systems, daily programme, full participant list, legacy timeline** are UNKNOWN — frame every one as "not yet announced." The pillar's credibility comes from saying this plainly.
- **Mascots, three zones, 45 timber pavilions, 8,000 events** are usable but partly REPORTED/non-official — attribute the non-official ones, don't state as organiser fact.
- **Never** invent a ticket price, a confirmed rail opening date, or a complete country list.
