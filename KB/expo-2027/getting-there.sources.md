# Sources — /expo-2027/getting-there

> Grounding source for the `getting-there` article. Derived from the Belgrade Expo 2027 Knowledge Base + verified web research (June 2026).
> Every fact carries a label: **CONFIRMED** (official Expo/government docs) · **REPORTED** (political statements, media, in-progress contracts) · **UNKNOWN** (not yet announced) · **CONFLICT** (sources disagree).
> The generator may state CONFIRMED facts plainly, must attribute REPORTED facts as provisional, must frame UNKNOWN as "not yet announced," and must explain CONFLICT rather than flatten it.
> Honor labels exactly. Do not state any REPORTED date or figure as settled.

## 1. Site location and distances

[CONFIRMED] The Expo 2027 Belgrade site is in the municipality of Surčin, southwest of central Belgrade.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participation Guide, 30 October 2024)

[CONFIRMED] The Expo 2027 Belgrade site is situated approximately 13.5 kilometres from the city centre and just 5 kilometres from Nikola Tesla International Airport.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participation Guide, 30 October 2024; restated in EXPO 2027 Participants FAQ, 13 December 2024)

[CONFIRMED] The Expo secure site covers 25 hectares; the total area including village, services and parking is 113 hectares.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participation Guide, 30 October 2024)

[CONFLICT] Official Expo documents give 13.5 km from the city centre, while a main contractor describes the site as "about 15 km from the center of Belgrade." The 13.5 km figure is the official planning value; "about 15 km" is a rounded contractor estimate. Both agree on 5 km to the airport.
(Sources: EXPO 2027 Participation Guide, 30 October 2024 [13.5 km]; Millennium Team project description, 29 September 2025 [about 15 km])

## 2. Getting from Nikola Tesla Airport to central Belgrade (current, 2026)

[CONFIRMED] As of 1 January 2025, all regular Belgrade city public transport — buses (including line 72 and its night variant 72N), trams, trolleybuses and BG Voz suburban trains — became free for all passengers, with no tickets, fares or validation required on regular city lines. (Policy current as of 2026; a fare policy is perishable and could be changed by a future administration — re-check closer to travel.)
(Source: Tourist Organisation of Belgrade (tob.rs), "City public transport" useful-info page, 2025; corroborated by multiple 2025–2026 reports that Belgrade made public transport free from 1 January 2025)

[CONFIRMED] City bus line 72 connects the central Zeleni venac terminal with Nikola Tesla Airport; a night variant, 72N, runs between Republic Square and the airport. As a regular city line it is free to ride (see above).
(Source: PlanPlus route 72, 17 September 2024; Moovit 72N schedule, 9 December 2025; TOB free-transport policy, 2025)

[REPORTED] Bus 72 is the cheapest way into the city — and since January 2025 it is free, as a regular city line. (The "cheapest" framing predates the free-transport policy; under current policy the regular bus simply costs nothing.)
(Source: Airmundo Belgrade Airport public transport guide, 14 December 2025; TOB free-transport policy, 2025)

[CONTEXT] Belgrade previously used a card-and-ticket fare system — BusPlus (from 2012), replaced by Beograd Plus in May 2023 — with tickets bought at kiosks, via app or by SMS. Since public transport became free in January 2025 there are no fares to pay on regular lines, and the app now serves route and schedule information rather than ticketing. This note exists only to explain why older guides still mention buying tickets or cards.
(Source: Beograd Plus / BusPlus system history; TOB free-transport policy, 2025; 2025–2026 traveller reports that ticketing was discontinued once transport became free)

[CONFIRMED] Airport minibus line A1 runs between Nikola Tesla Airport and Trg Slavija in the city centre, stopping in New Belgrade (Fontana). A1 is a separate paid shuttle service and is the exception to the free regular-transport policy.
(Source: PlanPlus official route listing for bus A1, 17 September 2024)

[REPORTED] The A1 minibus runs roughly every 15 to 30 minutes during the day; the on-board ticket costs about 400 RSD, paid in cash; the journey to Slavija takes about 30 to 40 minutes. (Fare is indicative for 2026 and perishable — it has risen over time and may change by 2027.)
(Source: Airmundo Belgrade Airport public transport guide, 14 December 2025)

[CONFIRMED] Nikola Tesla Airport operates an official taxi system with zone-based fixed prices: passengers take a fixed-price voucher from an e-kiosk, then proceed to the taxi stand, and the driver charges only the fare stated on the voucher.
(Source: Belgrade Nikola Tesla Airport official "Taxi service" page, 2025)

[REPORTED] An official airport taxi to the central zones costs roughly 2,200 to 2,800 RSD; an app-based CarGo ride costs roughly 1,500 to 2,500 RSD; the drive to the city centre takes about 25 minutes depending on traffic. (Belgrade has no Uber or Bolt; the main ride-hail apps are Yandex Go and CarGo. Fares are indicative for 2026 and perishable.)
(Source: AirportTransferPortal Belgrade Airport cost guide, 2026)

[REPORTED] The driving distance from the airport to the city centre (Trg Republike) is about 18 km, around 20 to 25 minutes by car via the E-70. (This is airport-to-downtown, not airport-to-Expo.)
(Source: Airmundo, 14 December 2025)

## 3. The new railway to the Expo area (Surčin)

[CONFIRMED] A new railway line is under construction from Zemun Polje to the National Stadium, passing Nikola Tesla Airport and Surčin. Its total length is 18.3 km, double-track, electrified, for regional passenger traffic, with stops at Singidunum, Nikola Tesla Airport and Surčin, and a station at the National Stadium.
(Source: Serbian Ministry of Construction, Transport and Infrastructure project page, published by 2025)

[CONFIRMED] The main contractor for the railway is Powerchina International Group; the contracting authority is the Government of Serbia via the Ministry of Construction, Transport and Infrastructure.
(Source: Serbian Ministry of Construction project page, 2025)

[REPORTED] The new railway is intended to link Nikola Tesla Airport, the National Stadium and the Expo complex with central Belgrade and the wider rail network; construction began in 2024.
(Source: Serbia Business / eKapija, 14 April 2024)

[REPORTED] Serbian officials, including the President and the Minister of Construction, have stated the railway is targeted for completion by the end of 2026, with one statement giving a date of 1 December 2026. More recent contractor and ministry reporting (2025–2026) describes the line as progressing toward an operational launch in spring 2027. These are officially stated targets, not a binding service timetable, and remain provisional.
(Sources: Serbian Monitor quoting the President, 27 October 2024; Serbia Business quoting Minister Aleksandra Sofronijević, 4 May 2025; EX-YU Aviation News, 21 February 2026 [spring 2027 framing])

[REPORTED] Officials have promised the line will allow a journey of about 15 minutes between the airport and central Belgrade (Prokop), with trains planned every 30 minutes and up to every 15 minutes at peak times.
(Sources: Serbian Monitor, 27 October 2024; EX-YU Aviation News, 8 May 2025)

[UNKNOWN] No detailed public passenger timetable, exact opening date, or fare scheme for this railway during the Expo period has yet been announced. (Note: with regular Belgrade transport currently free, whether the airport rail link will be free or carry a special fare is itself not yet specified.)
(Source: B92, 16 April 2025)

## 4. How visitors are expected to reach the Expo site during the event (15 May–15 August 2027)

[CONFIRMED] According to the Participants FAQ, visitors will access the site by multiple means: private vehicles, public buses, urban rail, and river transport. On-site parking accommodates up to 11,000 vehicles, with charging stations for e-bikes and scooters.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participants FAQ, 13 December 2024)

[CONFIRMED] The Participation Guide states that an urban railway will connect the Expo site to Belgrade's public transport network, public buses will serve site entrances A and C, and new environmentally friendly bus lines will connect the site with Nikola Tesla Airport, downtown Belgrade and surrounding municipalities. The Guide notes this information is preliminary and subject to update.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participation Guide, 30 October 2024)

[REPORTED] Serbia is procuring a fleet of electric buses for Expo 2027 to connect the Surčin site with locations across the city, with charging infrastructure at the Expo complex, the airport and major terminals; after the event the buses are reported to pass to GSP Belgrade for regular service.
(Sources: Sustainable Bus, 16 July 2025; Serbia Business / eKapija, 25 February 2025)

[CONFLICT] Early statements described 100 electric buses; a later tender and contract secured 50 electric buses plus 8 chargers. At least 50 are now contractually committed; the final deployed total may differ from the earlier figure.
(Sources: Serbia Business, 25 February 2025 [100]; Chariot Motors press release, 19 May 2026 [50 contracted])

[UNKNOWN] Final Expo-specific bus line numbers, routes and timetables between the city, rail stations and Expo entrances A and C for summer 2027 have not yet been published.
(Source: EXPO 2027 Participants FAQ, 13 December 2024)

## 5. Belgrade metro and Surčin

[REPORTED] The Belgrade Metro is under construction; officials have targeted Line 1 for completion around 2028 and Line 2 around 2030 — both after the Expo 2027 window. These are political statements, not contractual service dates.
(Sources: Telegraf / ministry coverage, 23 May 2023; Belgrade Metro overview)

[CONFIRMED] No planned metro line reaches Surčin, Nikola Tesla Airport or the Expo site. Rail access to the Expo zone is intended via BG Voz suburban rail, not the metro. A planned BG Voz line (Line 4), 16.2 km, is intended to run Novi Beograd–Nikola Tesla Airport–Surčin–National Stadium; it is a planned future line, not a service running today.
(Source: PUC "Belgrade Metro and Train" BG Voz development plans)

[UNKNOWN] There is no announced metro station or metro branch directly serving the Expo site or Surčin by 2027.
(Source: Belgrade Metro overview)

## 6. Key labels to honor when writing

- The **5 km to airport** and **13.5 km to city centre** distances are CONFIRMED and officially sourced — state plainly.
- **Belgrade public transport is free** on all regular city lines (buses incl. 72/72N, trams, trolleybuses, BG Voz) since 1 January 2025 — state plainly as current, but time-stamp it ("as of 2026") and treat the policy as perishable. Do NOT reintroduce old per-ticket fares (e.g. ~50 RSD single, ~120 RSD day, ~800 RSD week) — those predate the free-transport policy and are now incorrect.
- The **A1 minibus is the paid exception** (~400 RSD, cash, indicative/perishable) — keep it clearly distinct from the free regular buses.
- The **Beograd Plus / BusPlus card system** is historical context only — there are no fares to pay now; mention it only to explain why older guides talk about buying tickets.
- The **railway completion date (end of 2026 / spring 2027)** is REPORTED/provisional — must be attributed ("reported as…, not officially confirmed"), never stated as settled. This is the article's one §5 "never write as final" fact. Whether the rail link will be free or carry a special fare is UNKNOWN.
- **BG Voz Line 4** is a planned future line, NOT a currently running service — never describe it as operating today, and do not tie it to a fare system (none applies now).
- **Rail timetables, fares, and Expo shuttle routes** are UNKNOWN — frame as "not yet announced."
- The **number of electric buses (50 vs 100)** is a CONFLICT — explain both, do not flatten.
- **Current A1 fare, taxi fares and frequencies** are REPORTED/indicative for 2026 and may change by 2027 — present as indicative, not locked.
