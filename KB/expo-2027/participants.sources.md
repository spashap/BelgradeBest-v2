# Sources — /expo-2027/participants

> Grounding source for the `participants` article — "Participating countries: confirmed & expected." Derived from the Belgrade Expo 2027 Knowledge Base + freshness recheck of official sources (4 June 2026).
> Every fact carries a label: **CONFIRMED** (official Expo/BIE/government docs) · **REPORTED** (political statements, media, in-progress contracts) · **UNKNOWN** (not yet announced) · **CONFLICT** (sources disagree).
> The generator may state CONFIRMED facts plainly, must attribute REPORTED facts as provisional, must frame UNKNOWN as "not yet announced," and must explain CONFLICT rather than flatten it.
> Honor labels exactly. The participant count grows over time — never state it as a final total, and treat the public country list as partial.

## 1. The headline count (the page's core fact — recheck before every update)

[CONFIRMED] As of 4 June 2026, the official Participants page states that 137 nations have formally committed to participating in Expo 2027 Belgrade, with a note that more are coming.
(Source: Expo 2027 official Participants page, accessed 4 June 2026)

[CONFIRMED] The count has climbed steadily over time: ~94 (May 2025), 117–118 (mid-2025), 120 (Sept 2025), 137 (early–mid 2026). It is explicitly described as still growing.
(Source: Serbia Business, 2 May 2025; Expo 2027 "117 Countries" article, July 2025; "Facts and Figures" article; beinbelgrade.com, Sept 2025)

[CONFIRMED] This already exceeds the previous Specialised Expo — Astana 2017 — which hosted 115–117 participating countries, making Serbia a record holder for a Specialised Expo by participant count.
(Source: Expo 2027 "117 Countries" article, July 2025; Xinhua, 12 June 2025)

[CONFIRMED] Serbia's original stated target was 100 countries by end of 2025, later revised upward toward 120+; the actual count overshot both.
(Source: Serbia Business, 2 May 2025; Expo 2027 "117 Countries" article)

## 2. How participation works (the stable framework)

[CONFIRMED] Each participating country is expected to have a designated National Day and the opportunity to present itself through a pavilion, exhibitions, events, innovations, culture and cuisine.
(Source: Expo 2027 "Facts and Figures" article; official About page)

[CONFIRMED] The legal framework for participants is the Agreement on Privileges and Benefits signed between Serbia and the BIE, establishing customs/tax relief, the legal status of national commissioner offices, recognition of driving licences, and a "one-stop shop" for administrative formalities. Each participating country has a national commissioner and a legally recognised office in Serbia during the Expo.
(Source: Expo 2027 news, Agreement on Privileges and Benefits; Serbia Business coverage 2025)

[CONFIRMED] An "Expo visa," issued free of charge, allows accredited Expo participants/staff to live and work in Serbia for the Expo period. (This is for participants, NOT general visitors.)
(Source: Expo 2027 news, Agreement on Privileges and Benefits)

[REPORTED] Under the contracts, participating countries select pavilion locations and must establish legal entities, open bank accounts, hire staff and manage logistics such as importing exhibits and construction materials.
(Source: Serbia Business, 2 May 2025, quoting Director Dušan Borovčanin)

## 3. The public list (partial — handle honestly)

[CONFIRMED] The official Participants page lists a large subset of the 137 by name, but does not yet publish a complete, clean table of all participants; some names appear with duplicates in the page text.
(Source: Expo 2027 official Participants page, accessed 4 June 2026; KB §2)

[CONFIRMED] Named participants confirmed on the official list span every region — examples include China, Japan, South Korea, Türkiye, Greece, Austria, Switzerland, Hungary, Germany, Russia, Iran, Israel, Qatar, Azerbaijan, Uzbekistan, South Africa, Ethiopia, Montenegro, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Cuba, Venezuela, and many small island and African states.
(Source: Expo 2027 official Participants page country table, captured in KB §2)

[REPORTED] Non-official consolidations (e.g. beinbelgrade.com) aggregate a publicly confirmed list of roughly 80–120 countries, sometimes including states such as the United States, France, UAE, North Macedonia, Turkmenistan and Maldives where participation was announced but the official table did not yet display them. Use as cross-reference, not as the official record.
(Source: beinbelgrade.com participants list, Sept 2025; KB §2)

[UNKNOWN] The full public list of all 137 participants — including international organisations, cities and companies — is not yet completely published.
(Source: Expo 2027 official Participants page, accessed 4 June 2026)

## 4. Pavilions and country plans (mostly not yet revealed)

[CONFIRMED] The master plan by Fenwick Iribarren Architects organises the international participants into pavilions within the 25-hectare site, alongside three thematic pavilions and a Forum. Early design descriptions reference seven large pavilions housing participating countries plus an outdoor area of collapsible national pavilions.
(Source: Arquitectura Viva, FIA project description; KB §6/§7)

[REPORTED] A handful of countries have signalled pavilion plans:
 – Germany launched a public tender for its temporary pavilion (~€7.5 million), contributing under the sub-theme "Play for Progress."
 – Italy's foreign ministry and ambassador to Serbia have said Italy is preparing one of the largest pavilions, focused on people — meeting Italian athletes, musicians, researchers and students — built on five principles.
 – The UAE has confirmed participation, framing it as strengthening international cooperation.
 – Saudi Arabia signed its participation contract, planning a pavilion aligned with Vision 2030 (sport, culture, creative industries).
(Sources: Vreme, German tender; Esteri.it + Serbian Monitor, April 2026, Italy; Serbia Business, UAE; Saudi Arabia Breaking News, Saudi contract)

[UNKNOWN] Detailed architectural concepts, interior designs and themes for most national pavilions have not been publicly unveiled as of June 2026.
(Source: Vreme; KB §7 "what is not yet knowable")

[UNKNOWN] The schedule of National Days (which country on which date) has not been published.
(Source: Expo 2027 official Programme page, accessed 4 June 2026)
