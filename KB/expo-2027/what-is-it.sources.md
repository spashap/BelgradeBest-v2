## 1\. What kind of event this is

\[CONFIRMED] Expo 2027 Belgrade is a Specialised Expo, not a Universal (World) Expo. It is officially recognised by the Bureau International des Expositions (BIE), following Serbia's election as host and approval of its Recognition Dossier.
(Source: BIE / Planet Expo, 2024)

\[CONFIRMED] A Specialised Expo lasts three months and focuses on a specific theme, rather than the broader, larger scope of a Universal Expo.
(Source: Planet Expo / BIE, 2024)

\[CONFIRMED] Expo 2027 Belgrade is the first Specialised Expo in the Western Balkans.
(Source: BIE)

\[CONFIRMED] The Government of Serbia is the organiser and host country, working under BIE supervision. A government organising committee has been established.
(Source: Serbia Business, 2024)

## 2\. When and where

\[CONFIRMED] Expo 2027 runs from 15 May to 15 August 2027 — 93 days.
(Source: srbija2027.gov.rs; Expo 2027 official site)

\[CONFIRMED] The main site is in Surčin, in the western part of Belgrade, about 13.5 km from downtown and about 5 km from Nikola Tesla International Airport (per the official Participants Orientation FAQ).
(Source: Orientation27 Participants FAQ)

## 3\. The theme

\[CONFIRMED] The official theme is "Play for Humanity: Sport and Music for All," presenting play, sport and music as universal means of connection and progress.
(Source: Serbian Monitor; Expo 2027 official site)

\[CONFIRMED] The official site frames "Play for Humanity" as treating play — through sport, music, games and creative expression — as a catalyst for learning, innovation, social connection and global cooperation.
(Source: Expo 2027 official site)

## 4\. What visitors will find on site

\[CONFIRMED] The official concept describes three thematic pavilion units plus a Forum: the Power of Play Pavilion (science and psychology of play); the Play for Progress Pavilion (innovation driven by playful exploration); the Play Together Pavilion (sport, music and collaborative play); and the Forum (talks, panels, workshops, networking).
(Source: Expo 2027 official site)

\[CONFIRMED] Thematic programmes highlighted by the official site include "Music of all for all" (world music performances and workshops), "Sport of all for all" (global sports events and ambassadors of play), and "Limitless Play" (record-breaking challenges and interactive competitions).
(Source: Expo 2027 official site)

\[CONFIRMED] City-wide "Playgrounds" are planned as public-space interventions, including relaxation/spontaneous play zones, intergenerational play areas, and sustainability-themed installations.
(Source: Expo 2027 official site)

\[CONFIRMED] Each participating country is expected to have a designated National Day to present its culture, innovation and programme.
(Source: Expo 2027 official site)

\[UNKNOWN] As of June 2026, no full public daily calendar of concerts, sports events, conferences or ceremonies has been published — only high-level programme categories are described.
(Source: Expo 2027 official site)

## 5\. Scale — who's coming, how many visitors

\[CONFIRMED] As of June 2026, the official Participants page states that 137 nations have formally committed; the official site elsewhere refers to "over 120 nations," and a July 2025 release reported 117 confirmed. The number has grown over time and may continue to change.
(Source: Expo 2027 official site / Participants page)
\[CONFLICT/DRIFT] The exact count rises as more states confirm — attribute it to a date ("as of June 2026, 137"), never state a final number.

\[CONFIRMED – PROJECTION] The official Participants FAQ projects around 4.1 million visits over the 93 days, averaging about 44,652 per day with peaks up to around 89,304. This is a projection, not a count.
(Source: Orientation27 Participants FAQ)
\[CONFLICT] Other statements have referenced 2.6–3 million, and more recent ones "over four million." Projections range widely — treat as provisional, never as fact. (§5: never write visitor numbers as final.)

## 6\. Practicalities relevant to "is it worth visiting"

\[UNKNOWN] There is no announced special Expo-branded visa or automatic visa waiver for ordinary foreign visitors as of June 2026; visitors rely on Serbia's general visa policy. (The free "Expo visa" applies only to participants/staff, not general visitors.)
(Source: Expo 2027 official site / Agreement on Privileges and Benefits)

\[CONFIRMED] A government security framework is in place: statements describe security "at the highest possible level," an operations centre at the site, and engagement of around 20,000 volunteers. The Participants FAQ describes 24/7 monitoring, emergency response teams and safety drills.
(Source: srbija.gov.rs; Orientation27 Participants FAQ)

\[REPORTED] At COP29, Serbia presented a "Green Agenda for Expo 2027 Belgrade" ("Play GREEN, Play for HUMANITY"), highlighting sustainability and green technology cooperation as objectives.
(Source: Western Balkans Infohub)

## 7\. Labels to honor when writing

* Daily programme calendar, general-visitor visa specifics — UNKNOWN, frame as "not yet announced."
