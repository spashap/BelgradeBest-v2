## 1. The category (what this page covers)

[CONFIRMED] Belgrade's grill-and-street-food culture centres on roštilj (grilled meats), the pekara (bakery) tradition, and a strong late-night food scene. The core dishes are ćevapi, pljeskavica, and burek, alongside late-night pizza and gyros.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §10; Serbian cuisine sources)

[CONTEXT] This is the most affordable, highest-everyday-intent food content in the leg — what a foreign visitor actually eats between sit-down meals. The durable unit is the *dish and the venue type*; named spots are examples and churn.
(Source: leg archetype decision; Food & Nightlife research §10)

## 2. The core dishes

[CONFIRMED] **Ćevapi** (ćevapčići) are small grilled minced-meat sausages — beef, pork, lamb or a mix — typically served in or with flatbread (lepinja/somun), with chopped onions, and accompaniments such as kajmak and ajvar. Usually served in portions of several pieces.
(Source: Serbian cuisine sources; Food & Nightlife research)

[CONFIRMED] **Pljeskavica** is a large grilled minced-meat patty, the Serbian relative of a burger; often served with kajmak, ajvar and onions. The Leskovac style is a well-known spicy variant.
(Source: Serbian cuisine sources)

[CONFIRMED] **Burek** is a flaky filled pastry (meat, cheese or other fillings), a staple bakery and breakfast/late-night item, traditionally eaten with yoghurt.
(Source: Serbian cuisine sources; Food & Nightlife research §10)

[CONFIRMED] **Kajmak** (a clotted-cream dairy spread) and **ajvar** (a roasted red-pepper relish) are the defining accompaniments to grilled meats.
(Source: Serbian cuisine sources)

## 3. Venue categories (durable)

[CONFIRMED] The relevant venue types are: ćevabdžinica / roštilj spots (counter-service or quick-serve grilled meat), pekara (bakeries, found on nearly every corner, serving burek and pastries around the clock), and 24-hour / late-night spots serving grilled meat and burek after clubs close.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §8, §10; Serbian cuisine sources)

## 4. Late-night and 24-hour food (durable culture, perishable venues)

[CONFIRMED] Belgrade has a strong late-night food culture centred on burek, pizza, gyros and grilled meats; 24-hour bakeries serving burek are a standard post-club option.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §10)

[REPORTED] **Trpković** bakery is repeatedly cited by locals and travellers as a reference point for high-quality burek — durable-leaning by sustained, independent mention. Named as an established example; current hours/locations perishable.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §10; multiple guides)

[REPORTED] Some burek houses advertise early-20th-century founding dates (e.g. "since 1908"); such long-standing bakeries are durable-leaning institutions, though exact founding details should be treated as reported, not verified.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §10)

[REPORTED] Late-night gyros, pizza-by-the-slice and 24-hour bakeries are widely documented as the standard after-club food; specific named burger/pizza spots are mostly perishable single-location businesses — describe the category, name sparingly.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §10)

## 5. Price mechanics (perishable — indicative only)

[REPORTED] Belgrade grill and street food is consistently described as very affordable by European-capital standards — a grill or bakery meal is among the cheapest ways to eat in the city. Specific prices fluctuate; treat any figure as indicative as of 2026 and direct readers to current menus.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §9.3; Serbian cuisine sources)

[REPORTED] Cash matters more here than in sit-down dining: bakeries, kiosks and some small grill spots may be cash-only, even as cards are accepted across most of the city — carry some dinars. (Indicative posture, as of 2026.)
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §9.1)

## 6. Practical notes for foreigners

[REPORTED] In smaller neighbourhood grill spots and bakeries, menus may be Serbian-only and English limited; touristic areas (Knez Mihailova, Skadarlija, the riverfront) more reliably have bilingual menus. Learning a few food keywords helps.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §12.1)

[REPORTED] Accidental vegetarian options exist (cheese burek, some bakery items), but vegetarians should clarify ingredients — hidden animal fats and cheese are common, and grill spots are meat-centred.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §12.2)
