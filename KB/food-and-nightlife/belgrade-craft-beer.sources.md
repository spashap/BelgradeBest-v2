## 1. The scene

[CONFIRMED] Belgrade is widely described as Serbia's craft-beer capital, with multiple microbreweries, taprooms and specialist beer pubs clustered mainly around Dorćol, Savamala and central neighbourhoods.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §5)

[CONTEXT] This is a listicle: it recommends specific breweries and taprooms. Discipline — lean on the flagship/durable breweries; flag the volatile bar segment as perishable; each venue a discrete checkable fact for future revalidation.
(Source: leg archetype decision; Food & Nightlife research §5)

## 2. The flagship (durable anchor)

[REPORTED] **Dogma Brewery** — a flagship microbrewery founded in 2016 in a renovated sugar-factory building near the Sava and Ada Ciganlija, with an on-site taproom and beer garden opened in 2017. Durable-leaning (multi-year operation, widely cited; the scene is mature enough to support themed brewery tours visiting it).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §5)

[REPORTED] **Samo Pivo** — a long-standing central beer bar, frequently recommended. Durable-leaning.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §5)

## 4. Domestic beer context

[REPORTED] Beyond craft, the popular mainstream domestic beers are Jelen and Lav; on the craft side, domestic labels such as Kabinet and Dogma are commonly recommended (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: discovery sources; Food & Nightlife research §5)

## 5. The experience

[CONFIRMED] The craft-beer scene is a distinct going-out mode — taprooms and beer gardens, concentrated in the Dorćol/central cluster, with the scene mature enough to support guided brewery tours.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §5)

[REPORTED] Beer pricing context: at clubs/splavovi beer has been reported roughly 2–4 EUR; taproom pricing varies (indicative, as of 2025–2026 — check current menus).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §9.3)
