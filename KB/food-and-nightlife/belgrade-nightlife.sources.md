## 1. The scene at a glance

[CONFIRMED] Belgrade's nightlife is a recognised draw in its own right, spanning floating river clubs (splavovi), bar districts in former industrial quarters, bohemian kafana streets, and a late, every-night-of-the-week going-out culture.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §1, §7)

[CONTEXT] This anchor orients a foreign visitor — the kinds of nights available, the districts, the seasonality and the rhythm — and cross-links to the deep-dive pages for specifics. It is not a venue list.
(Source: leg archetype decision)

---

## 2. Seasonality (the orienting fact)

[CONFIRMED] The scene is explicitly seasonal: roughly May–September the high-energy nightlife moves to the riverbank splavovi; in winter it concentrates in indoor clubs and bars. A visitor's options differ sharply by season.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §7.1, §12.4)

---

## 3. The kinds of night

[CONFIRMED] The main formats: **splavovi** (floating river clubs — the signature summer scene); **bar districts** (Cetinjska/Dorćol, Savamala, Beton Hala — warm-up bars, cocktail bars, converted-warehouse venues); **kafanas** (traditional taverns with live music and rakija — the bohemian, sit-and-stay night); and a **specialist drinks scene** (rakija bars, wine bars, craft-beer taprooms).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §7, §8, §11; discovery scan)

[REPORTED] Belgrade is widely described as going out every night of the week, not just weekends, with clubs commonly running until roughly 04:00–06:00 and some later (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §9.4; discovery scan)

---

## 4. The district map (character only)

[CONFIRMED] **Savamala / riverfront** is the splav and electronic-music heartland; **Cetinjska and Dorćol** hold the bar/cocktail and craft-beer cluster; **Skadarlija** is the kafana quarter; **Beton Hala** is the polished waterfront dining-and-drinks strip. Each district has a distinct nightlife personality.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §11)

---

## 5. The practical shape (orienting, not the full guide)

[CONFIRMED] Reservations are effectively required for large-capacity clubs and splavovi, especially on weekends; dress code at upscale venues is smart-casual minimum; the legal drinking age is 18.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §9.5, §9.6)

[REPORTED] Cash matters at smaller/older venues while cards are accepted across most of the scene; entry is often free but seated tables carry minimum spends — "free entry" is not a cheap night at a prime table (indicative postures, as of 2026).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §9.1, §9.3)
