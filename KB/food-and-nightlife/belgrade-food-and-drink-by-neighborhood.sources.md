## 1. What this page does

[CONFIRMED] Belgrade's food and nightlife cluster by district, each with a distinct eating-and-drinking personality: Skadarlija (kafanas), Dorćol (coffee, wine, craft beer, bistros), Savamala/riverfront (splavovi, electronic music), Beton Hala (waterfront dining), Zemun (riverside fish and kafanas), Vračar (neighbourhood dining and cafés), New Belgrade (hotel and business dining).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §11)

[CONTEXT] This page maps the *food/drink* character of each district and cross-links the format-based pages (kafanas, splavovi, coffee, wine, beer) and the cuisine anchor. It does NOT re-explain what each district is — that's Visit Belgrade's territory.
(Source: leg archetype decision; leg IA decision)

## 2. Skadarlija (Stari Grad)

[CONFIRMED] Skadarlija's food-and-drink identity is almost entirely kafanas — long traditional dinners, live tamburica music, rakija. Visitors come for the kafana experience rather than bars or clubs; coffee and dessert are secondary.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §11.1)

---

## 3. Dorćol

[CONFIRMED] Dorćol's food-and-drink identity centres on small cafés and specialty coffee, wine and craft-beer bars, plus modern bistros and street-food spots. It is the city's coffee-and-craft cluster.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §11.2)

---

## 4. Savamala and the riverfront

[CONFIRMED] Savamala's food-and-drink identity is nightlife-led — it is the splav and electronic-music heartland, with large river clubs; food here is secondary to the going-out scene (casual grills, street food, some riverfront restaurants).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §11.3)

---

## 5. Beton Hala (Sava waterfront)

[CONFIRMED] Beton Hala's identity is a polished waterfront dining-and-drinks strip in a converted warehouse — Serbian and international restaurants with river views, cocktail bars, and venues that blend dinner into late-night.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §11.4)

---

## 6. Zemun

[CONFIRMED] Zemun's food-and-drink identity leans to riverside fish restaurants, river specialities and relaxed kafana-style dining along the Danube; its nightlife is calmer than central splavovi — long dinners rather than clubs. Zemun also has its own green market.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §11.5)

[CONTEXT] Zemun as a *place/town* and what to see there is owned by Visit Belgrade (`zemun`). This page covers only its eating/drinking character — cross-link for the rest.
(Source: leg scope/IA decision)

---

## 7. Vračar

[CONFIRMED] Vračar's identity is residential and café-dense — neighbourhood restaurants, brunch spots and dessert shops, around the Temple of Saint Sava.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §11.6)

---

## 8. New Belgrade (Novi Beograd)

[CONFIRMED] New Belgrade's food-and-drink identity is hotel and business dining — large business-hotel restaurants and rooftop lounges with international menus and river/skyline views — more than traditional kafana culture.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §11.7, §13)
