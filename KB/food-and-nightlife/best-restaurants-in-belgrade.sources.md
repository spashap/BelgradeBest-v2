## 1. The scene

[CONFIRMED] Belgrade has a maturing fine-dining and contemporary-Serbian scene. The Michelin Guide introduced a dedicated Belgrade selection in 2022, signalling the city's arrival on the international dining map; a "New Balkan" wave reinterprets traditional dishes with seasonal local sourcing and modern plating.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.1, §2.2)

[REPORTED] By the 2024 edition the Michelin Belgrade selection highlighted around 22 restaurants, including Bib Gourmand value picks, spanning contemporary Serbian, Mediterranean, Asian and international cuisines (indicative, as of the 2024 selection — Michelin selections change yearly; check the current edition).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.1)

## 2. Durable anchors (multi-year, multiply-cited — safe to recommend)

[REPORTED] **Salon 1905** — fine dining in a historic Art Nouveau building, Serbian/international, repeatedly Michelin-listed since the first Belgrade selection. Durable (historic building, sustained recognition).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.1)

[REPORTED] **Iva New Balkan Cuisine** — the flagship of the modernised-Balkan approach, with a Michelin Bib Gourmand for value; regional ingredients, inventive plating. Durable (Bib Gourmand, strong coverage).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.1, §2.2)

[REPORTED] **Klub Književnika** — a post-WW2 literary café turned restaurant, over 70 years of history, now Michelin-listed. Durable (decades-old institution).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.1)

[REPORTED] **Langouste** — long-running upscale seafood with river views near Kosančićev venac, Michelin-listed. Durable (long-running specialist).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.1)

[REPORTED] **Comunale Caffè e Cucina** (Beton Hala) — shared-plates Italian on the riverfront, popular for years, Michelin-listed. Durable (prime location, longstanding).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.1)

[REPORTED] **Enso** — contemporary tasting-menu/seasonal modern-European, multi-year guide presence, chef-driven. Durable-leaning.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.1)

[REPORTED] **Istok** — Vietnamese bistro with a 2024 Bib Gourmand, praised for authentic generous dishes; durable as a concept though more niche cuisine.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.1)

## 3. The wider listed field (mixed durability)

[REPORTED] The broader Michelin-listed and well-regarded field includes Homa, Magellan, The Square, Ebisu, Metropolitan, SkyLounge, Na Ćošku, Mezestoran Dvorište, Bela Reka, Gušti Mora, JaM, Legat 1903, and others — creative bistros, hotel fine dining, seafood and new-Balkan concepts. Many have operated for years (durable-leaning); newer entries (e.g. The Twenty Two, L'Adresse) should be treated as perishable-leaning until they prove longevity.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.1, §2.2)

[REPORTED] **Business/investor dining** clusters in hotel restaurants and rooftop lounges (e.g. SkyLounge, Metropolitan Grill, The Square), concentrated in New Belgrade and central hotels — useful for business dinners (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §13)

## 4. New Balkan — the durable theme

[REPORTED] The "New Balkan" movement (Iva as flagship, with places like Legat 1903, Na Ćošku, Bela Reka integrating regional/village recipes into refined menus) is the durable, distinctive theme — institutions that pair local ingredients with long ownership are more durable than chef-branded pop-ups or management-tied hotel restaurants.
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.2)

## 5. Practicalities (perishable — indicative only)

[REPORTED] Fine-dining and Michelin-listed restaurants strongly recommend reservations, especially weekends; dress is smart-casual to elegant. Many serious restaurants double as wine venues showcasing Serbian regions. Prices are perishable — direct readers to current menus (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: Food & Nightlife research §2.2, §9.5; §4)
