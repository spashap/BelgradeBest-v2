## 1. Climate and seasonality

[CONFIRMED] Belgrade has a continental/humid-subtropical climate: warm-to-hot summers, cold winters, rain spread across the year. Historical July averages sat around 21.5 °C, rising toward ~23.8 °C since the mid-1980s, reflecting summer warming; recent analyses note a further warming trend over the last decade. Annual precipitation is around 690–700 mm. (Physical trends are stable; exact monthly averages move slowly.)
(Source: deep research §7.1, citing climatology data)

## 2. Tourist seasons

[REPORTED] Seasonal pattern (stable):
 – **Peak: late May–September.** Warm to hot, busy nightlife and festivals, higher accommodation prices and more crowding.
 – **Shoulder: April–early June and late September–October.** Milder, fewer crowds, lower prices — attractive for city breaks.
 – **Winter: November–March.** Colder, shorter days; Christmas/New Year events and ski trips elsewhere in Serbia draw regional visitors, but Belgrade city visits are quieter except around holidays.
(Source: deep research §7.2)

[REPORTED] For sightseeing comfort, the shoulder months (April–May, September–October) offer the best balance of pleasant weather and thinner crowds; summer suits festival-goers and nightlife but brings heat and peak prices.
(Source: deep research §7.2, §7.4)

## 3. Expo 2027 timing (the cross-link to the Expo leg)

[CONFIRMED] Expo 2027 Belgrade runs 15 May–15 August 2027 — overlapping almost exactly with Belgrade's hottest months (June–August) and the peak domestic holiday season.
(Source: deep research §7.3, citing BIE/Expo documentation)

[REPORTED] Authorities expect several million visitors during the Expo window; hotel sources anticipate strong accommodation demand and upward price pressure, with new hotel capacity being added ahead of 2027. Expect heat, crowding and elevated prices in summer 2027; booking early and considering nearby cities (e.g. Novi Sad) as a base may help. (Directional trend stable; exact magnitudes perishable.)
(Source: deep research §7.3, §7.4, §9.2)

[REPORTED] Visitors whose main goal is Belgrade sightseeing rather than the Expo itself might prefer the pre- or post-Expo shoulder months (April–early May or September–October 2027) to enjoy upgraded infrastructure without peak-event pressure.
(Source: deep research §7.4)
