## 1. The headline: the old downtown stations are gone

[CONFIRMED] Belgrade's historic main railway station near the riverfront closed in 2018. Beograd Centar — colloquially "Prokop" — is now the de facto main railway station, though parts of it remain unfinished.
(Source: deep research §8.2, citing station references)

[CONFIRMED] The old central bus station (BAS) near the former railway station ceased operations in September 2024. Long-distance buses now use a new main station in Block 42 (Antifašističke borbe street) in New Belgrade, adjacent to the New Belgrade railway station. At the time of research the new station building was not fully complete; a temporary administrative building handled ticketing and operations.
(Source: deep research §8.2)

[CONTEXT] Because older guides, maps and blogs still reference the old downtown stations, visitors frequently go to the wrong place. This page's main job is to correct that and explain where to actually go now.
(Source: deep research §8.2, §10.4)

## 2. Reaching the railway station (Prokop / Beograd Centar)

[CONFIRMED] Prokop is connected to the city by several bus lines — notably line 36 linking it to Slavija and Savski Trg, plus lines 34, 38A, 40, 41 and 44 — and by BG Voz suburban trains. (All free under the city's free-transport policy; see getting-around.)
(Source: deep research §8.2; cross-ref getting-around §1)

[REPORTED] Bus 600 from the airport serves Prokop directly, making it the natural arrival route for travellers connecting straight to a train. (Free; airport-to-city detail lives on that page.)
(Source: deep research §1.3; cross-ref airport-to-city §2)

## 3. Reaching the bus station (Block 42, New Belgrade)

[CONFIRMED] The new main bus station is in Block 42 in New Belgrade, next to the New Belgrade railway station. Reaching Block 42 and Prokop can be confusing for newcomers because both are away from the old downtown locations. (Reachable via the free city network; exact best lines are perishable — point readers to a live planner.)
(Source: deep research §8.2, §10.4)

[REPORTED] The station building was still being completed at the time of research, with a temporary building handling ticketing and operations — so the on-the-ground experience may change. Perishable.
(Source: deep research §8.2)

## 4. Onward travel to other Serbian cities

[REPORTED] From these stations, travellers can reach other Serbian cities (e.g. Novi Sad, Niš) by intercity bus and by rail, including the Belgrade–Novi Sad high-speed line. Buy tickets at the station ticket windows or operator channels. Specific routes, operators, frequencies and fares are perishable — give the how, not fixed schedules.
(Source: deep research §8.2; general intercity context)

[CONTEXT] Note for older-guide readers: a small platform-access fee historically applied at the old bus station (cash). Whether and how such fees apply at the new Block 42 station is perishable — flag as "check on arrival" rather than stating a figure.
(Source: deep research, intercity station context)
