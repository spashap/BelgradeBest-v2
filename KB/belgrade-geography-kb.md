# Belgrade Geographic Knowledge Base

Coverage date: 2026-06-20

LEGEND
- type: Category of place (neighborhood, district, municipality, square, street, promenade, park, riverfront, landmark, museum, venue, transit-node, market, lodging-area, medical-cluster, day-trip).
- municipality: One of Belgrade's city municipalities in which the location lies.
- coordinates: Latitude and longitude in decimal degrees (WGS84), used for map pins.
- position: Relative location in Belgrade using well-known anchors and basic distances.
- borders: Adjacent neighborhoods or areas that touch or clearly bound the location.
- character: One–two sentence summary of atmosphere, built form, and who the area suits.
- known_for: Comma-separated tags capturing notable traits and uses.
- key_streets: Key streets, boulevards, or sub-areas inside the location.
- points_of_interest: Notable sights, parks, landmarks, or facilities within or on the edge.
- access: Key transit access, walkability notes, plus airport distance where relevant.
- visitor_use: Typical ways visitors and relocators use the area (sightseeing, base, nightlife, errands).
- lodging_fit: Who should consider staying here and what the main trade-offs are.
- label: CONFIRMED (cartographic/official) or REPORTED (guide/blog/secondary, or containing perishable details).
- source: Main source or sources used plus URL and access date.



### Republic Square | Трг Републике
- type: square
- municipality: Stari Grad
- coordinates: 44.8164, 20.4601
- position: Central Belgrade; eastern end of Knez Mihailova, ~0.3 km SE of Kalemegdan Park
- borders: Knez Mihailova, Vase Čarapića, Francuska, Čika Ljubina
- character: Major central square and orientation point framed by monumental 19th–20th century buildings; busy, urban, and suited to first-time visitors who want to be in the thick of things.
- known_for: central-square, meeting-point, cultural-institutions, pedestrian-core, nightlife-access
- key_streets: Knez Mihailova, Kolarčeva, Francuska, Vase Čarapića
- points_of_interest: National Museum, National Theatre, Prince Mihailo Monument
- access: Dense web of city bus and tram routes at Trg Republike and nearby Terazije; ~17 km by road from Nikola Tesla Airport via E75 and Gazela bridge
- visitor_use: Starting point for walking tours, meeting point, gateway to old town sights and shopping streets.
- lodging_fit: Excellent base for first-time visitors who prioritize walkability and centrality over quiet; can be noisy and busy late.
- label: REPORTED
- source: Dook International – Republic Square, Belgrade (https://www.dookinternational.com/poi/republic-square/7346), Trip.com guide to Republic Square (https://www.trip.com/moments/theme/poi-republic-square-61190016-comprehensive-guides-993136/), accessed 2026-06-20

### Knez Mihailova Street | Улица Кнез Михаилова / Ulica kneza Mihaila
- type: street
- municipality: Stari Grad
- coordinates: 44.8178, 20.4569
- position: Main north–south pedestrian spine between Republic Square and Kalemegdan, ~0.2–0.8 km south of the fortress plateau
- borders: Republic Square area, Kalemegdan Park, surrounding Stari Grad grid
- character: Historic pedestrian shopping and promenade street with late 19th- and early 20th-century townhouses, chain stores, and cafes; highly touristic and always busy.
- known_for: pedestrian-street, shopping, cafes, architecture, streetlife
- key_streets: Kneza Mihaila, Čika Ljubina, Obilićev venac passages
- points_of_interest: Historic facades, galleries, branches to Belgrade City Library and cultural centers
- access: Short walk from Republic Square transit hub; ~18 km from Nikola Tesla Airport by car via E75 and city center streets
- visitor_use: Core walking axis linking main sights; casual shopping, people-watching, and access to side-streets with cafes and bars.
- lodging_fit: Strong for visitors who want a pedestrianized base directly on the main tourist street, with trade-off of noise and crowds.
- label: REPORTED
- source: Latitude.to – Knez Mihailova Street (https://latitude.to/satellite-map/rs/serbia/47991/knez-mihailova-street), Belgrade.tips – Knez Mihailova Street (https://belgrade.tips/index.php/2020/12/23/knez-mihailova-street-all-roads-lead-to-it/), accessed 2026-06-20

### Belgrade Fortress / Kalemegdan | Београдска тврђава / Калемегдан
- type: landmark
- municipality: Stari Grad
- coordinates: 44.8233, 20.4503
- position: At the confluence of the Sava and Danube rivers, on a ridge NW of Republic Square (~0.9 km) and Knez Mihailova (~0.6–0.8 km)
- borders: Upper Dorćol, Lower Dorćol, Sava riverfront, Danube riverfront
- character: Historic citadel and surrounding park spanning upper and lower town, with panoramic river views; a must-see scenic and historic anchor rather than a residential area.
- known_for: fortress, park, river-views, history, museums, walking
- key_streets: Tadeuša Košćuška, Bulevar vojvode Bojovića, Pariska
- points_of_interest: Fortress walls and gates, Kalemegdan Park, Military Museum, Victor Monument, Belgrade Zoo (on the lower slopes)
- access: Walkable from Republic Square and Knez Mihailova; nearby tram and bus stops along Bulevar vojvode Bojovića and Tadeuša Košćuška; ~19 km from Nikola Tesla Airport by road
- visitor_use: Major sightseeing area for views, history walks, and park time; evening strolls and sunset watching over the confluence.
- lodging_fit: Visitors usually stay in nearby Stari Grad or Dorćol rather than inside the park; ideal to have walking access rather than be directly adjacent.
- label: REPORTED
- source: PeakVisor – Belgrade Fortress (https://peakvisor.com/poi/belgrade-fortress.html), TourBySpot – Kalemegdan Park, Belgrade Fortress (https://www.tourbyspot.com/spots/kalemegdan-park-belgrade-fortress-belgrade-serbia), accessed 2026-06-20

### Stari Grad (Old Town district) | Стари Град
- type: district
- municipality: Stari Grad
- coordinates: 44.8190, 20.4590
- position: Central-right bank district covering the historic core between the Sava–Danube confluence and the Terazije–Slavija ridge; includes Republic Square, Knez Mihailova, and much of the traditional city centre.
- borders: Dorćol, Skadarlija, Vračar, Savski Venac, Danube riverfront, Sava riverfront
- character: Dense historic urban fabric with a mix of 19th–20th century buildings, institutions, and nightlife streets; highly walkable and strongly oriented to visitors and services rather than family-scale green space.
- known_for: historic-core, government-buildings, culture, nightlife, tourism, pedestrian-areas
- key_streets: Knez Mihailova, Cara Lazara, Francuska, Kneza Miloša edge, Bulevar despota Stefana
- points_of_interest: Republic Square, Belgrade Fortress/Kalemegdan, Skadarlija, Dorćol streets, key museums and theatres
- access: Multiple tram and bus lines criss-cross the district; easy walking access between most central anchors; ~18 km by road from Nikola Tesla Airport via E75.
- visitor_use: Primary sightseeing and strolling district, with many accommodation options, restaurants, bars, and cultural venues.
- lodging_fit: Ideal base for first-time visitors and short stays wanting maximum walkability; trade-offs are traffic, noise, and limited greenery compared with outlying residential areas.
- label: REPORTED
- source: Stari Grad municipality overview (https://www.starigrad.org.rs), various cartographic sources accessed via OpenStreetMap, accessed 2026-06-20

### Skadarlija | Скадарлија
- type: neighborhood
- municipality: Stari Grad
- coordinates: 44.8178, 20.4643
- position: ~0.3 km east of Republic Square and just north-east of Terazije; a short downhill walk from the square.
- borders: Upper Dorćol, Cetinjska area, Republic Square fringe, Stari Grad grid
- character: Cobblestoned bohemian quarter built around Skadarska Street, with traditional kafanas and live music venues, preserving a late-19th/early-20th century ambience.
- known_for: bohemian-quarter, kafanas, cobblestone-street, live-music, nightlife, traditional-cuisine
- key_streets: Skadarska, Zetska, Cetinjska, Strahinića Bana fringe
- points_of_interest: Historic kafanas and restaurant courtyards, murals and street art, small galleries
- access: 5–8 minute walk from Republic Square and Terazije; served indirectly by numerous central bus and tram lines on surrounding boulevards.
- visitor_use: Evening dining and live-music destination, plus daytime strolling for atmosphere and photography.
- lodging_fit: Suits visitors who want a characterful base near nightlife and old-town sights; cobbles, late noise, and crowds can be drawbacks for light sleepers.
- label: CONFIRMED
- source: Skadarlija – coordinates and description (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skadarlija; https://latitude.to/articles-by-country/rs/serbia/32050/skadarlija), accessed 2026-06-20

### Dorćol (Upper) | Дорћол (Горњи Дорћол)
- type: neighborhood
- municipality: Stari Grad
- coordinates: 44.8200, 20.4660
- position: Immediately north and north-east of Republic Square, sloping toward the Danube; upper Dorćol generally refers to the area closer to the city centre, above Cara Dušana.
- borders: Stari Grad core, Skadarlija, Lower Dorćol, Danube riverfront
- character: Mixed-residential and commercial inner-city streets with cafes, small shops, and cultural venues, popular with younger residents and expats.
- known_for: inner-city-living, cafes, mixed-use-streets, proximity-to-river, culture
- key_streets: Dobračina, Visokog Stevana, Strahinjića Bana upper section, Cara Dušana (boundary toward lower Dorćol)
- points_of_interest: Dorćol Platz arts center area, small galleries and bars
- access: 10–15 minute walk from Republic Square; served by bus and tram lines along Cara Dušana and Bulevar despota Stefana.
- visitor_use: Good for experiencing everyday central Belgrade with easy access to both the historic core and riverfront.
- lodging_fit: Attractive to medium- to long-stay visitors and remote workers seeking a more local feel within walking distance of the centre; streets can be busy and parking constrained.
- label: REPORTED
- source: Dorćol neighborhood description and coordinates (https://tripomatic.com/en/poi/dorcol-poi:33353; https://wikimapia.org/7290460/Dor%C4%87ol), accessed 2026-06-20

### Dorćol (Lower) | Дорћол (Доњи Дорћол)
- type: neighborhood
- municipality: Stari Grad
- coordinates: 44.8233, 20.4669
- position: Slopes down from upper Dorćol toward the Danube; roughly the area below Cara Dušana down to the riverfront, east of the fortress.
- borders: Upper Dorćol, Danube riverfront, Belgrade Fortress lower slopes, industrial/port zones further east
- character: Quieter, more residential grid close to the Danube with a mix of older low-rise and newer infill buildings, sports and recreation facilities, and some warehouses.
- known_for: riverside-proximity, residential-blocks, sports-facilities, quieter-streets
- key_streets: Dunavska, Venizelosova, Braće Baruh, Tadeuša Košćuška lower sections
- points_of_interest: Sports courts and riverfront paths, access to the Danube quay areas
- access: 15–20 minute walk downhill from Republic Square or via tram and bus along Bulevar vojvode Bojovića and Cara Dušana.
- visitor_use: Suits visitors who want to be near the centre but closer to the river and slightly away from the busiest tourist streets.
- lodging_fit: Good for medium stays valuing river access and a residential feel; distances back uphill to main sights and limited retail on some blocks are the main trade-offs.
- label: REPORTED
- source: Dorćol area mapping via OpenStreetMap and Mapcarta (https://mapcarta.com/N4773685799; https://wikimapia.org/7290460/Dor%C4%87ol), accessed 2026-06-20

### Savamala | Савамала
- type: neighborhood
- municipality: Savski Venac
- coordinates: 44.8144, 20.4532
- position: On the right bank of the Sava just south-west of the old town ridge; ~0.8–1.0 km south-west of Republic Square, stretching between Branko bridge and the old Sava port.
- borders: Stari Grad (above the slope), Belgrade Waterfront zone, Sava riverfront, Savski Venac uplands
- character: Former industrial and warehousing waterfront district that became known for creative spaces, nightlife, and riverfront clubs, now intersecting with large-scale Belgrade Waterfront redevelopment.
- known_for: riverfront, nightlife, creative-spaces, warehouses, redevelopment
- key_streets: Karađorđeva, Travnička, Gavrila Principa, the lower Savski kej
- points_of_interest: Historic warehouses, cultural venues in converted buildings, access to Sava riverbank splav zones
- access: Short walk downhill from central Stari Grad and the Branko bridge area; trams and buses run along Karađorđeva; ~17 km from Nikola Tesla Airport via E75 and Gazela.
- visitor_use: Nightlife and creative-scene destination plus a transition between the historic core and new riverfront developments.
- lodging_fit: Appeals to visitors prioritizing nightlife and proximity to the riverfront; surroundings are more urban/industrial in feel and can be noisy, especially in season.
- label: CONFIRMED
- source: Savamala coordinates and description (https://www.latlong.net/place/savamala-belgrade-serbia-15144.html; https://mapcarta.com/es/W1285099304), accessed 2026-06-20
