## 1. Why Belgrade for dental

[REPORTED] Dental work is the flagship of Serbian medical tourism — sector estimates attribute up to ~80% of foreign health-tourism demand to dental services, drawing patients chiefly from the diaspora and Western Europe for implants, prosthetics and cosmetic dentistry (indicative, as of 2025).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §1, §2.1 — Tier a estimates)

## 2. What dental tourists come for

[REPORTED] The common treatments sought are dental implants (single, multiple, full-arch/All-on-X), crowns and veneers, and broader cosmetic dentistry ("smile makeover" work). These are the same procedures explained clinically on the general anchor page (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.1; MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (dental) — Tier a/b)

## 3. Belgrade cost reality (perishable — Tier-b)

[REPORTED] Indicative Belgrade implant pricing runs roughly €350–€800+ per implant, with one aggregator citing ~€390–€815 across many clinics and premium systems higher (indicative, as of 2024–2026; Tier-b clinic/price-list data — confirm current quotes).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §3.1 — Tier b)

[REPORTED] Key transparency point: some clinics price only the implant fixture at insertion and bill the abutment, crown and any bone graft/sinus lift separately months later — which can double or more the headline per-implant cost. A quoted "implant price" may not be the cost of a finished tooth (indicative, as of 2024–2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §3.1 — Tier b)

[CONFLICT] On savings: clinics/facilitators advertise headline savings of 70–80% vs Western prices; semi-official sources cite ~50–70%; neutral cost-comparison analysis puts the realistic all-in saving (after flights, accommodation, home scans, return visits) closer to 20–60%. Present the honest all-in basis, not the marketing figure.
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.1, §3; MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (dental cost) — Tier b promotional vs cost-comparison)

## 4. The trip pattern (durable)

[REPORTED] Implant treatment is typically staged: an initial visit for assessment/placement, a healing period of several months for osseointegration, then a return for the abutment and final crown — so dental implant tourism often means two trips or a long gap, not a single visit. Simpler work (crowns, veneers, cleaning) can be done in one trip. Confirm the staging with the provider (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (dental process); MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.1 — Tier a/b)

## 5. Vetting and aftercare (cross-link, brief)

[REPORTED] Dental tourists should apply the same vetting as any treatment abroad — verify the dentist's credentials and the clinic's standards, clarify exactly what the quote includes, and confirm what happens if a problem arises after returning home (e.g. a failed implant, a warranty that requires returning). Continuity of care matters: a home dentist may be reluctant to take over work done elsewhere (indicative posture, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §5; MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (risk) — Tier a)
