## 1. What IVF is (tier-a)

[CONFIRMED] IVF (in vitro fertilisation) is assisted reproduction in which eggs are retrieved after ovarian stimulation, fertilised with sperm in a laboratory (including ICSI, where a single sperm is injected into an egg), and resulting embryos are cultured and transferred to the uterus; surplus embryos may be frozen (vitrified) for later transfer.
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (IVF, process) — Tier a)

## 2. What a cycle involves (tier-a)

[CONFIRMED] A typical cycle involves: ovarian stimulation with hormone medication and monitoring; egg retrieval under sedation; fertilisation in the lab (conventional IVF or ICSI); embryo culture over several days; embryo transfer; and a waiting period before a pregnancy test. Frozen embryo transfer (FET) uses previously vitrified embryos in a later cycle.
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (IVF, process) — Tier a)

## 3. Success rates — realistic and age-banded (tier-a — the moat)

[REPORTED] Success rates decline significantly with the woman's age when using her own eggs, and a single cycle is often not successful — multiple cycles are common. Clinics may quote favourable figures; the honest picture is that per-cycle live-birth rates are strongly age-dependent and lower than headline marketing implies. Quoted success rates should be read carefully (what they measure: per-cycle vs per-transfer, clinical pregnancy vs live birth) (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (IVF, success rates) — Tier a)

[CONTEXT] State success realistically and age-banded; the trust/verification axis penalises echoing inflated clinic success claims. Note that how a rate is defined changes the number.

## 4. Common add-ons (tier-a)

[REPORTED] Headline IVF prices rarely include the full picture: medication, ICSI, PGT (preimplantation genetic testing), vitrification/storage, and separate embryo-transfer/FET fees are common add-ons that can substantially raise the real cost. Some add-ons have limited evidence of improving live-birth rates for general patients — they should be weighed critically (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (IVF, add-ons) — Tier a)

## 5. International cost comparison (perishable — Tier-b)

[REPORTED] A single IVF cycle (before add-ons) ranges roughly €1,800–€5,200 across popular destinations, with Western markets (UK, Germany, Australia, US) generally higher and lower-cost European hubs less; headline package prices commonly exclude medication, PGT, vitrification, storage and separate transfer fees, so the realistic total can be considerably higher (indicative, as of 2026; Tier-b cost-comparison data).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (IVF, price comparison) — Tier b)

## 6. What marketing omits (the honest core — tier-a)

[REPORTED] The key misunderstandings: a single cycle often isn't enough; success is strongly age-dependent and lower than headline figures suggest; quoted "success rates" vary by definition and aren't comparable across clinics; and the headline price is rarely the all-in cost once medication and add-ons are included. An honest guide states these plainly (indicative posture, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (IVF, what marketing omits) — Tier a)
