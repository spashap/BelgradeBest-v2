## 2. The procedures (faithful — tier-a)

[REPORTED] **LASIK/PRK** reshape the cornea to correct refractive error; not everyone is a candidate (depends on cornea, prescription, eye health), and documented risks include dry eye, glare/halos and, less commonly, under/over-correction needing enhancement. **Cataract surgery** replaces the clouded lens with an artificial intraocular lens and is a common, generally high-success procedure, with risks including infection and posterior-capsule issues. State candidacy and risks faithfully (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (eye surgery) — Tier a)

## 3. Belgrade cost bands + follow-up (perishable — Tier-b)

[REPORTED] Indicative Belgrade pricing at one eye hospital: cataract surgery with lens implantation from ~€1,500 per eye; laser refractive correction ~€1,000 — with limited free-follow-up windows (e.g. ~30 days after surgery, ~6 months for refractive). Follow-up windows matter for a travelling patient and are a key thing to confirm (indicative, as of 2026; Tier-b price list — confirm current quotes).

## 4. Travel caution (durable)

[REPORTED] As with any procedure abroad, post-operative review and any complication tend to arise after the patient has left; eye surgery needs follow-up checks, so the trip should allow the required post-op review before travelling home, and the reader should know where to seek urgent eye care if needed (indicative posture, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (risk); MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §5 — Tier a)
