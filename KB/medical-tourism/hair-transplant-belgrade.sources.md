## 1. Belgrade's positioning

[REPORTED] Belgrade is marketed as a value MID-PRICE hair-transplant destination — cheaper than Western Europe but typically more expensive than Turkey (the high-volume low-cost leader). Specialised Belgrade clinics publish per-graft pricing and English content, indicating sustained foreign demand (indicative, as of 2026).

## 2. Belgrade cost bands (perishable — Tier-b)

[REPORTED] Indicative all-inclusive Belgrade FUE ranges: ~€1,900 for up to ~1,500 grafts; ~€2,800–€3,800 for ~1,500–3,000 grafts; ~€3,900–€5,200 for 3,000+ grafts — usually including the surgery and local transfers, sometimes a hotel, but excluding flights. One clinic lists ~€2,000–€3,000 per 1,000 grafts and body-hair transplant ~€4/graft (indicative, as of 2026; Tier-b marketing — confirm current quotes).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.5 — Tier b)

[REPORTED] "All-inclusive" packages tend to downplay extras — repeat sessions if more density is wanted, medication, PRP, and long-term follow-up are often not in the headline price (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.5; MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (hair transplant, what marketing omits) — Tier a/b)

[CONFLICT] Serbia-vs-Turkey value: marketing frames Belgrade as premium-quality value; international cost comparisons group Serbia as mid-price, not the cheapest tier (Turkey/India lower per-graft). Present the realistic mid-price positioning, not "best value" marketing.
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (hair transplant comparison) — Tier b cost-comparison)
