## 1. Why Belgrade is a recognised centre

[REPORTED] Belgrade is repeatedly cited as an established international centre for gender-affirming and complex genital/urogenital reconstructive surgery, with Belgrade surgeons receiving referrals of complex cases from multiple countries. One association-sourced figure (2019) reported roughly 70–100 such operations a year in Belgrade for patients from North America, ex-Yugoslavia, Italy, Israel, and the Middle East. Treat the count as an indicative, dated estimate, not a current registry figure (as of 2019/indicative).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.3 — Tier a association figure + Tier b centre material)

## 2. The procedures (what-to-expect level — tier-a)

[REPORTED] The main procedures associated with this field include chest/"top" surgery (masculinising or feminising) and genital/lower surgery such as phalloplasty, metoidioplasty and vaginoplasty. ## 3. Staged surgery and the long-stay reality (durable — the key practical fact)

[REPORTED] Genital/lower reconstructive surgery is frequently staged — multiple operations over time rather than a single procedure — and requires extended stays and careful aftercare. Belgrade providers' own guidance, for example, recommends staying at least around two weeks after a first-stage procedure, with some patients medically obliged to stay longer, and stresses flexible return travel. This long-stay, multi-stage reality is the central practical fact for anyone planning travel (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.3 — Tier b centre logistics, treated as empirical trip-length data)
