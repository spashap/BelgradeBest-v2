## 1. What this page covers

[CONTEXT] Two things: (a) elective orthopedics (hip/knee replacement, sports) — a real but secondary foreign draw; and (b) an honest account of cardiology, oncology and bariatric care — present in Serbia but NOT leading destinations, with advanced cases often handled elsewhere. The honesty in (b) is the page's value.
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.7, §2.8 (article implications) — Tier a)

## 2. Elective orthopedics

[REPORTED] Serbia has a strong orthopedic ecosystem with heavy domestic demand and long public waiting lists (e.g. tens of thousands on hip/knee endoprosthesis waiting lists in mid-2025, nearly half in Belgrade institutions) — a signal of capacity, though that figure is domestic-patient data, not foreign-patient volume. Private Belgrade orthopedic hospitals market hip/knee replacement and sports orthopedics to international patients, emphasising minimally-invasive and "one-day surgery" pathways (indicative, as of 2025).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.7 — Tier a/b)

[REPORTED] **Hip and knee replacement** (faithful, tier-a): established procedures with generally good outcomes, but major surgery with real recovery and rehab timelines (weeks to months), and standard surgical/anaesthetic risks. Rehab is substantial — Serbian rehabilitation spas (e.g. described durations of ~4–6 weeks after knee, ~4 weeks to 3 months after hip) offer post-operative programmes (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (hip/knee); MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.7 — Tier a)

[CONTEXT] The travel-and-recovery caution is acute here: major joint surgery + long rehab + DVT-risk-from-flying means the trip and recovery must be planned carefully, and continuity of care home is a real issue. Cross-link risks/aftercare.
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (risk) — Tier a)

## 3. The honest limits — cardiology, oncology, bariatric (the moat)

[REPORTED] **Cardiology:** named in Serbian medical-tourism promotion, but there is no strong independent evidence that foreign patients choose Belgrade specifically for cardiology over more established centres; most such claims are promotional (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.8 — Tier a/b)

[REPORTED] **Oncology:** Serbia's national cancer institute collaborates with foreign proton-therapy centres (in Italy) to send patients abroad for some advanced treatment — underscoring that for certain cutting-edge oncology, Serbs themselves travel out. There is no robust evidence Belgrade is a net inbound oncology destination (indicative, as of 2025).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.8 — Tier a)

[REPORTED] **Bariatric/weight-loss surgery:** little high-quality evidence of dedicated bariatric tourism into Serbia; European bariatric comparisons rarely feature Serbia, and where it's mentioned it's within broader plastic-surgery marketing (indicative, as of 2025).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.8, §1 — Tier a/b)

[CONTEXT] State these limits plainly and honestly — that for advanced serious-disease care, Serbia is often NOT the destination and Serbian patients themselves travel abroad. This candour is exactly what marketing sites omit and is the page's distinctive value. Do not pad these into seeming like strong inbound draws.
(Source: leg honesty/profile decision)
