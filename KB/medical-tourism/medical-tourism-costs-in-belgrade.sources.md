## 1. How to read a medical quote (durable)

[CONFIRMED] A quoted "price" or "package" is not the all-in cost. The structure that matters: what the headline figure covers vs. what's billed separately, and what travel/living costs sit on top. This framework is stable even as the numbers change.
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §3; MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (cost sections) — synthesis)

[REPORTED] Common hidden or separately-billed items documented across procedures: in dental, the implant fixture may be priced at insertion while the abutment, crown and any bone graft are billed separately later — which can double or more the headline implant cost. In surgery, "all-inclusive" often means medical elements (theatre, anaesthesia, one hospital night) only, with extra nights billed (e.g. ~€100/night reported) and consultations sometimes free only if you proceed. "Free accommodation" terms vary (clinic apartment vs hotel; number of nights) (indicative, as of 2024–2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §3.1, §3.2 — Tier b clinic price lists)

## 2. The savings reality (the honest core — CONFLICT)

[CONFLICT] How much you actually save is contested. Clinics and facilitators commonly advertise savings of 70–80% versus Western prices; semi-official Serbian sources cite ~50–70% on common procedures. But these compare headline procedure prices and often cherry-pick high Western comparators. More neutral cost-comparison analysis finds the realistic all-in saving — after flights, accommodation, home scans, medications and any return visits — is closer to 20–60% depending on procedure and country. Present all of these, and state that the all-in, neutral figure (20–60%) is the more honest basis for a decision; never flatten to the marketing number.
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.1, §3 (50–70%, 70–80% marketing); MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (dental cost, "realistic 20–60%") — Tier b promotional vs Tier b cost-comparison)

## 3. Indicative Belgrade price bands by procedure (all Tier-b, perishable)

[REPORTED] All figures below are indicative, Tier-b marketing/price-list data, time-sensitive (2024–2026), and exclude flights and most accommodation unless stated. They are for orientation only — confirm current quotes directly. Each procedure page covers its own pricing in detail.
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §3 — Tier b)

[REPORTED] **Dental implants:** roughly €350–€800+ per implant across Belgrade clinics (one aggregator citing ~€390–€815 across many clinics), with premium systems higher; abutment/crown/grafting often separate (indicative, as of 2024–2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §3.1 — Tier b)

[REPORTED] **Cosmetic/plastic surgery (examples):** breast augmentation roughly €3,200–€5,100; rhinoplasty roughly €2,500–€3,400; abdominoplasty roughly €2,500–€4,000, with packages sometimes including a hospital night and follow-up but not travel (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §3.2 — Tier b)

[REPORTED] **Hair transplant (FUE):** indicative all-inclusive Belgrade ranges roughly €1,900 for up to ~1,500 grafts, €2,800–€3,800 for ~1,500–3,000, and €3,900–€5,200 for 3,000+, often including local transfer/sometimes hotel but excluding flights and ongoing medication (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.5 — Tier b)

[REPORTED] **IVF:** clinic price lists around 220,000–360,000 RSD per cycle, typically excluding medications and some tests — mid-range European pricing, not deep discount (indicative, as of 2024–2025).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.4 — Tier b)

[REPORTED] **Eye surgery:** at one Belgrade eye hospital, cataract surgery with lens from ~€1,500/eye and laser refractive correction ~€1,000, with limited free-follow-up windows (e.g. 30 days; 6 months for refractive) — indicative, as of 2026.
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.6 — Tier b)

## 4. The international comparison (why people travel — perishable)

[REPORTED] For context, the same procedures cost substantially more in high-cost countries — e.g. single dental implants commonly ~£1,850–£3,000 (UK) or ~$3,000–$6,000 (US); rhinoplasty often £7,500–£11,000 (UK) or $10,000–$20,000 (US). Belgrade/Serbia generally sits as a mid-price destination — cheaper than Western Europe, typically pricier than Turkey for the same advertised work (indicative, as of 2025–2026; figures are Tier-b comparison data).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (per-procedure price comparisons) — Tier b cost-comparison)

## 5. Total cost of the trip (durable principle)

[REPORTED] The honest budgeting principle: add to the medical quote the cost of flights, accommodation for the full required stay (including recovery before safe travel), local transport, any pre-trip scans/tests at home, medications, and a contingency for extra nights or a return visit if revision is needed. The all-in figure is what determines real savings (indicative posture, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (cost sections); MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §3 — Tier a/b)
