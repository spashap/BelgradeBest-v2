## 1. The Belgrade cosmetic-surgery picture

[REPORTED] Cosmetic/plastic surgery is the next most in-demand foreign category after dental; Belgrade clinics market the common procedures (rhinoplasty, breast augmentation, abdominoplasty, liposuction, facelift, gynecomastia) to international patients with English-language sites and packages (indicative, as of 2025).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.2 — Tier a/b)

[REPORTED] Serbia is realistically a MID-PRICE destination — clearly below Western Europe, but typically pricier than Turkey or Poland for the same advertised work. It is not the cheapest tier; frame it as mid-price, not "cheap miracle" (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §2.2; MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (cosmetic price comparison) — Tier a/b)

## 2. The common procedures (faithful, risk-honest — tier-a)

[REPORTED] **Rhinoplasty** reshapes the nose (open/closed approaches); recovery involves bruising/swelling that can take months to fully settle, and tier-a reviews report meaningful revision rates (around 10–20% in some techniques) and possible persistent functional issues — outcomes are constrained by the patient's own anatomy, not just surgical skill (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (rhinoplasty) — Tier a)

[REPORTED] **Breast augmentation** (implants above/below muscle, various incisions): documented complications include capsular contracture (broader literature ~4–10%, higher in smokers/revisions), malposition, rupture, sensation changes and possible revision surgery (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (breast augmentation) — Tier a)

[REPORTED] **Abdominoplasty (tummy tuck), liposuction, facelift** are offered; all carry standard surgical risks (bleeding, infection, scarring, anaesthetic risk, need for revision) and recovery timelines that must allow safe travel home (indicative, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (cosmetic sections) — Tier a)

[CONTEXT] State risks faithfully — the trust/verification rubric penalises minimised risk framing. Do not present cosmetic surgery as low-risk or its outcomes as guaranteed.
(Source: leg profile decision)

## 3. Belgrade cost bands (perishable — Tier-b)

[REPORTED] Indicative Belgrade examples: breast augmentation ~€3,200–€5,100; rhinoplasty ~€2,500–€3,400; abdominoplasty ~€2,500–€4,000. Some clinics state the price includes anaesthesia, theatre, surgical team and one hospital night with follow-up, extra nights ~€100/night, and an initial consultation free only if you proceed; facilitators may add "free accommodation"/transfers whose terms vary (indicative, as of 2026; Tier-b — confirm current quotes).
(Source: MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §3.2 — Tier b)

[CONFLICT] Savings vs Western prices are real (Belgrade is well below UK/US/Australia for comparable advertised procedures) but the headline figures are marketing; the honest all-in saving after travel and extras is lower (the costs page covers the 20–60% all-in reality). Don't echo "X% cheaper" as fact.
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (cosmetic comparison); MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §3 — Tier b)

## 4. The travel-and-recovery caution (durable)

[REPORTED] Cosmetic surgery + travel carries a specific risk: flying soon after surgery raises blood-clot (DVT) risk and can strain healing or disturb fresh incisions. Recovery time before flying home must be planned, and complications often appear after return when the operating surgeon is far away (indicative posture, as of 2026).
(Source: MedicalGeneralResearch-2.md (risk); MedicalResearch-Belgrade.md §5 — Tier a)
