## 1. The must-see anchors (at a glance)

[CONFIRMED] The canonical first-timer must-sees are: Belgrade Fortress (Kalemegdan), the Temple of St. Sava, the Nikola Tesla Museum, Skadarlija, Republic Square + Knez Mihailova, Ada Ciganlija, Zemun/Gardoš Tower, and Avala Tower (just outside the city). These recur across essentially all reputable guides.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3; multiple 2026 guides — Viator, Tripadvisor, Time Travel Turtle)

[CONTEXT] This page's edge over OTA listicles is the "why it matters" framing per site, not the list itself — the list is commodity, the interpretation is the differentiator. Practical detail is deliberately thin here and linked out, so the page stays an overview.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §A.3)

## 2. The sites and why they matter

[CONFIRMED] **Belgrade Fortress (Kalemegdan)** — the promontory above the Sava–Danube confluence with layered Roman-to-Austrian fortifications; grounds open 24/7 and free, best confluence panorama in the city. Where you physically see Belgrade's whole layered history. (Full fortress practicals — seasonal hours, per-site tickets, the combined-ticket question — on `understanding-belgrade`.)
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3, §E.1)

[CONFIRMED] **Temple of St. Sava** — one of the largest Orthodox churches in the world; marble façade, mosaic interior, huge dome. As of 2026, open daily ~08:00–20:00, free entry (donations). Anchors modern Serbian Orthodox identity; monumental interior. Hours perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3, §E.1; 2026 coverage citing the temple's posted hours)

[CONFIRMED] **Nikola Tesla Museum** (Vračar) — Tesla's archive plus live electrical demonstrations; the canonical "science" stop. As of 2026: Mon 10:00–18:00, Tue–Sun 10:00–20:00; English guided tour ~800 RSD; cash only, no cards; hourly ~45-min tours. (Full museum detail on `belgrade-museums`.)
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2; official tesla-museum.org, accessed 2026-06-06)

[REPORTED] **Skadarlija** — cobblestone bohemian quarter (kafanas, live music), "Belgrade's Montmartre"; pedestrian and always open, venues set their own hours. The easiest plug into "old Belgrade" via food and atmosphere.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3, §C.4)

[CONFIRMED] **Republic Square + Knez Mihailova** — the main square (National Museum, National Theatre, Prince Mihailo statue) and the adjoining pedestrian promenade to the fortress; public, 24/7. The everyday civic stage of the city.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3)

[CONFIRMED] **Ada Ciganlija** — the Sava lake/beach peninsula, "Belgrade's sea," with paths, cafés and sports; a free-access city park effectively open 24/7, with facilities and lifeguards active May–September. Note: the 07:30–15:30 hours on the official Ada site are office hours, not park access. Shows how Belgraders use the rivers in summer.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3, §E.1; TOB; adaciganlija.rs)

[CONFIRMED] **Zemun + Gardoš Tower** — the Austro-Hungarian river town with Danube views and a signature tower. (Tower hours are a CONFLICT — see the `zemun` page; link there rather than stating fixed hours.)
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3, §E.1)

[CONFLICT] **Avala Tower** — a TV-tower viewpoint on a low mountain ~16 km south, with the Monument to the Unknown Hero. Hours conflict across sources: 09:00–18:00 (a business directory) vs 09:00–20:00 (Apple Maps / Belgrade-Beat). Treat 09:00–18:00 as the conservative baseline and summer-to-20:00 as reported; advise verifying by phone/TOB. Tickets reported ~200/100 RSD (VIP ~400) but not officially confirmed for 2026 — indicative only. A half-day escape with a regional-landscape view.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.1; PlanPlus; Apple Maps; Belgrade-Beat; kasadoo 2025-12)

## 3. Practical framing

[REPORTED] Most guides agree Belgrade's central sights cluster within a walkable core (Republic Square → Knez Mihailova → Kalemegdan, plus Skadarlija), with Vračar (St. Sava, Tesla) a short hop and Zemun/Ada/Avala as half-day add-ons. With public transport free since January 2025, moving between them carries no fare. (Trip-length and budget detail on `belgrade-itinerary-3-days` and `is-belgrade-worth-visiting`.)
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.5, §E.4)
