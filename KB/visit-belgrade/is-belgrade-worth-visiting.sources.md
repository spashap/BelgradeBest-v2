## 1. The case (at a glance)

[CONTEXT] The honest "worth it" answer flips the common doubt ("isn't Belgrade just grey concrete / not pretty?"): yes, it's worth visiting precisely because it's layered rather than conventionally pretty — the crossroads history, the visible succession of empires, and the Brutalist/socialist heritage are the draw, not a drawback.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §A.2, §A.3)

[CONFIRMED] Belgrade reliably delivers a short city break: a few days cover the main sights, neighbourhoods and a strong food/nightlife and arts scene, with the rivers (Ada Ciganlija, the Danube) as a summer dimension. Tourism has been rising — Belgrade drew ~1.38 million visitors in 2023 (+17% YoY), and Serbia 4.43 million tourists in 2024 with Belgrade ~29.3% of overnight stays.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §A.5, §C.5; Belgrade Tourist Organisation via Cord Magazine 2024; RZS 2025)

## 2. Is Belgrade safe?

[CONFIRMED] Belgrade is generally safe for visitors, with low violent crime; the U.S. State Department notes violent crime is mostly tied to organized crime and hooliganism around high-profile sporting events, not tourists. The main visitor risks are petty crime (pickpocketing in crowds) and taxi overcharging. Crime indices are moderate (≈37.8 in 2026).
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.4; US State Dept Serbia page 2025-04; Statbase 2026; Numbeo)

[REPORTED] Solo and night-time visits, including for solo female travellers, are widely described as manageable with standard precautions — stick to central/well-lit areas, avoid post-derby football-crowd hotspots, and be alert with taxis. Risk category is stable; specific tactics perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.4; traveller summaries 2022–2025)

## 3. Is Belgrade cheap?

[REPORTED] Belgrade is affordable but no longer ultra-cheap (prices have risen since ~2020). Rough daily budget bands as of 2026: budget ~$50/day (~5,000 RSD; hostel/cheap apartment + local food), mid-range ~$150–170/day (hotel, restaurants, some paid sights), comfort/luxury $250–450+/day. Indicative/perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.4; GoTripzi 2026; BudgetYourTrip 2025; Numbeo 2026)

[CONFIRMED] Several major draws are free: the fortress grounds, the Temple of St. Sava, Ada Ciganlija, and — since 1 January 2025 — all city public transport. Many museums also have a monthly free-admission day. This keeps a low-budget trip genuinely viable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.1, §E.2, §E.4)

## 4. When to go

[CONFIRMED] Best months are April–June and September–October — mild weather, festivals, fewer extremes than mid-summer (July–August can be very hot).
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.4; ClimatesToTravel)

[CONFIRMED] Expo 2027 runs 15 May–15 August 2027 (specialised Expo, ~4–4.1 million visitors expected, 130+ countries) and will intensify summer crowds and prices that year — visitors not attending Expo may prefer the shoulder months in 2027.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §A.5, §E.4; expobelgrade2027.org)

## 5. Getting around, money, language

[CONFIRMED] Public transport (buses, trams, trolleybuses) is free for all passengers since 1 January 2025. Taxis: use apps (Bolt-equivalents like CarGo/Yandex) or official ranks and confirm the meter — taxi overcharging is the most-cited scam. (Airport-to-city detail lives on the Plan Your Trip leg.)
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.4; B92 2024-12-17)

[CONFIRMED] Currency is the Serbian dinar (RSD) only — euros are not accepted in shops, though cards are widely accepted and ATMs are common. English proficiency is high (EF EPI rank ~25, "high proficiency"); visitors generally manage in English alone.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.4; Serbian money guide 2025; EF EPI Serbia fact sheet 2025)
