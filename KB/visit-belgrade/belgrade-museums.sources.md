## 1. The top-tier set (at a glance)

[CONFIRMED] The core visitor museums are the Nikola Tesla Museum, the National Museum of Serbia, the Museum of Contemporary Art (MoCAB), the Museum of Yugoslavia (with the House of Flowers / Tito's tomb), the Military Museum, and the Ethnographic Museum. Secondary: the Historical Museum of Serbia, Natural History Museum, and the (commercial) Museum of Illusions.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2; TOB "Museums and Arts"; Tripadvisor rankings)

[CONTEXT] Consolidating these into one page is a single-source win — museum hours/prices change and are best maintained in one place; other pages link here. Many of these museums also run a monthly free-admission day, which is worth surfacing for budget visitors.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2, §E.4)

## 2. Nikola Tesla Museum (Vračar)

[CONFIRMED] Holds Tesla's personal archive and runs live electrical demonstrations on mandatory ~45-minute guided tours (Serbian and English, starting hourly). As of 2026: Monday 10:00–18:00, Tuesday–Sunday 10:00–20:00. English guided tour 800 RSD single / 500 RSD per person for groups (>10); Serbian tours 400 / 250. Payment is cash only in RSD — credit cards are not accepted. Individuals need no reservation; groups (>10) must book ahead. Hours/prices perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2; official tesla-museum.org "Plan your visit," accessed 2026-06-06)

[CONTEXT] The cash-only rule is a practical gotcha worth stating plainly — visitors arriving card-only have been caught out. (CHANGE 24–26: Monday is now open and the daily schedule clarified; older third-party "closed Monday" claims are stale.)
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2)

## 3. National Museum of Serbia (Republic Square)

[REPORTED] Serbia's flagship art/archaeology collection, on Republic Square. Hours: Tuesday/Wednesday/Friday/Sunday 10:00–18:00, Thursday/Saturday 10:00–20:00, Monday closed. Tickets: permanent exhibition 300 RSD, temporary 500 RSD, combined 600 RSD; students 50% off, under-7 free; free admission on Sundays. Hours/prices perishable; confirm at narodnimuzej.rs.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2; 2024 visitor guide consistent with 2018 official reopening data)

[CONFLICT] One aggregator (TripTap) lists Monday–Saturday open / Sunday closed — this contradicts the well-established "closed Monday, free Sunday" pattern and is likely incorrect. Honor the closed-Monday/free-Sunday version; treat the aggregator schedule as wrong.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2)

## 4. Museum of Contemporary Art (MoCAB / MSUB), Ušće

[CONFIRMED] Flagship modernist museum building at Ušće (Block 15), reopened 2017. As of 2026: Wednesday/Friday/Sunday 11:00–19:00, Thursday/Saturday 12:00–20:00, Monday 11:00–19:00, closed Tuesdays. Ticket 600 RSD (300 reduced), with several free categories. Free admission the first Wednesday of each month for all visitors. Hours/prices perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2; official msub.org.rs, 2025-07-15; free-day event listing 2026-02)

[CONTEXT] MoCAB doubles as a Brutalism/modernist-architecture stop — the `brutalist-belgrade` page references it; keep the facts identical there and here.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.2, §E.2)

## 5. Museum of Yugoslavia + House of Flowers (Tito's tomb)

[CONFIRMED] The interpretive core for the Yugoslav period, comprising the House of Flowers (Josip Broz Tito's mausoleum), the 25 May Museum and the Old Museum. As of 2026: Tuesday–Sunday 10:00–18:00, closed Mondays. Tickets: 800 RSD full; 600 RSD Serbian residents; 200 RSD resident students/pupils/pensioners; 400 RSD non-resident students. Free admission the first Thursday of each month, on national holidays, and on 4 and 25 May; under-10s and people with disabilities free. Hours/prices perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2; official muzej-jugoslavije.org "Visit us," 2026-03-25)

[CONTEXT] This is the high-value net-new museum for the leg — it's where the Yugoslav/Tito chapter of Belgrade's story becomes a physical visit; pairs naturally with `understanding-belgrade`'s socialist layer.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2, §C.1)

## 6. Military Museum (Kalemegdan)

[CONFIRMED] In Kalemegdan, covering Serbian and regional military history (with outdoor hardware in the fortress park). As of 2026: Tuesday–Sunday 10:00–17:00, last ticket 16:00, closed Mondays and on specified holidays (1–2 Jan, 7 Jan, 1–2 May, 11 Nov). Tickets: adults 350 RSD, students/pupils 250, family 500, with group discounts. Hours/prices perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2; official muzej.mod.gov.rs visitor info)

## 7. Ethnographic Museum

[CONFIRMED] One of the region's notable ethnographic collections, central location. As of 2026: Thursday–Tuesday 10:00–20:00, closed Wednesdays; ticket sales stop 30 minutes before closing. Permanent-exhibition tickets: adults 600 RSD, reduced 300, family (up to five) 700, with group rates; guided-group option ~1,000 RSD per group. Free Mondays reported. Hours/prices perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.2; official etnografskimuzej.rs "Visit us," 2025-05-26)
