## 1. What Zemun is (at a glance)

[CONFIRMED] Zemun was under Habsburg / Austro-Hungarian rule from 1717 until 1918, developing as a separate frontier town across the Danube from Ottoman-then-Serbian Belgrade; it was incorporated as a Belgrade municipality in 1934. It preserves an 18th–19th-century street fabric — low Austro-Hungarian and Balkan-style houses, churches and a riverside quay — and reads as an "old soul" district distinct from central Belgrade.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.1, §C.4; Old Downtown of Zemun overview; Belgrade My Way "Zemun Old Town")

[CONTEXT] Zemun is the city's most legible Austro-Hungarian layer — where the "Habsburg frontier" chapter of Belgrade's layered history is physically intact rather than fragmentary. That's its differentiator for a visitor.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.1)

## 2. Gardoš / Millennium Tower

[CONFIRMED] The Gardoš (Millennium) Tower on Gardoš Hill was built in 1896 to mark 1,000 years of Hungarian settlement in the Pannonian plain; it is Zemun's signature landmark and a Danube viewpoint.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3, §C.4)

[CONFLICT] Opening hours for the tower are reported inconsistently across sources as of 2026: local Serbian-language blogs give 10:00–19:00 in summer; major OTAs (Trip.com) list 10:00–16:00; the Waze live feed shows 10:00–18:00 (Tue–Sun). No authoritative municipal/museum micro-site with current official hours surfaced. Treat 10:00–16:00 as a minimum baseline, note likely extended summer hours (to ~18:00–19:00), and advise readers to verify on the day (posted sign at the tower or local tourist office). Do not present any single set of hours as settled.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.1; Trip.com; Waze 2026-06; Kofer.info 2021; Kuda sa decom 2024)

[REPORTED] There is a small entrance fee (scattered sources suggest roughly 150–200 RSD, children less), but no current official price schedule surfaced — exact 2026 fee is UNKNOWN. If mentioned, frame as "a small local entry fee," not a fixed figure.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.1)

## 3. What a visitor does in Zemun

[CONFIRMED] Zemun works as a half-day or evening: walking the old-town streets and the Danube quay, climbing Gardoš for the view, and the area is known for fish restaurants along the river. It offers a smaller-town rhythm a short distance from central Belgrade.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3, §C.4; Humbo "Zemun – old soul of Belgrade," 2024)

[CONTEXT] Zemun is reachable within the city; with public transport free since 1 January 2025, getting there carries no fare (see `is-belgrade-worth-visiting` / itinerary for transport detail). Keep this transport fact consistent with the other Visit Belgrade pages.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.4; B92 2024-12-17)
