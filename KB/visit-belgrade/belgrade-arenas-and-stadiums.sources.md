## 2. The venues (evergreen spine)

[CONFIRMED] **Belgrade Arena / Štark Arena** — the main multi-purpose indoor arena, in New Belgrade (Bulevar Arsenija Čarnojevića 58), capacity 18,386 seats (configurable ~2,000–20,000), 70 luxury boxes, 791 parking places. Primary basketball tenants are KK Partizan and KK Crvena zvezda. No standing public-tour product is documented — visits are for events.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.3; official starkarena.co.rs "About us"; tenant status REPORTED)

[CONTEXT] The arena's name has shifted with sponsors (Belgrade Arena → Kombank → Štark → Belgrade Arena again from 2024) — mention both common names so visitors recognise it under either.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.3)

[CONFIRMED] **Rajko Mitić Stadium ("Marakana," FK Crvena zvezda)** — capacity ~53,000, opened 1963, ~3.5 km from the centre. Offers guided stadium tours (dressing rooms, tunnel, media room, VIP areas, club museum); no tours on match days; bookings via the club, and local operators bundle it.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.3; StadiumGuide; More Than Belgrade)

[REPORTED] **Partizan Stadium (Stadion Partizana / JNA)** — FK Partizan's ground; tours (museum, locker rooms, tunnel, pitch) are offered via local operators. Capacity is commonly cited around 29,000–30,000 but was not freshly confirmed from an official 2024–2026 source — treat the figure as approximate.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.3; tour-operator pages)

[CONFIRMED] **Aleksandar Nikolić Hall (Pionir)** — an ~8,000-seat indoor hall (opened 1973), the city's main basketball hall before the arena. **Tašmajdan Sports Centre** — a historic sports/aquatics complex (Olympic pool, ~2,000-seat stands) that hosts aquatics events and open-air events.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.3; Belgrade-Beat; European Aquatics)

## 3. The events layer (perishable — freshness-monitored)

[CONFIRMED] **There is NO EuroBasket in 2026.** The 2025 men's EuroBasket was held outside Serbia (Cyprus/Finland/Latvia/Poland), and the next edition is EuroBasket 2029 (Estonia/Greece/Slovenia/Spain). Any "EuroBasket 2026 in Belgrade" framing is false — do not write it.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.3; FIBA event guides)

[CONFIRMED] **FIBA World Cup 2027 European Qualifiers** run in windows; those touching September 2026–September 2027 are 24 Aug–1 Sep 2026, 23 Nov–1 Dec 2026, and 22 Feb–2 Mar 2027. Serbia is expected to play home games (likely in Belgrade), but exact match dates, venues and opponents are not yet published.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.3; FIBA "How to qualify," 2026-03)

[UNKNOWN] Whether Belgrade hosts a EuroLeague Final Four in the 2026–27 season is not decided — Belgrade has reportedly bid, but no host has been announced.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.3; basketnews 2024)

[CONFIRMED] One confirmed in-window event: a UFC Fight Night at Belgrade Arena on 1 August 2026.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.3; Koobit)

[UNKNOWN] The 2026–27 Eternal Derby (Crvena zvezda–Partizan) fixture dates and the Belgrade Arena concert calendar for the window are not yet published — league and venue schedules are released in waves; mark these as not-yet-announced and re-check.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.3)
