## 1. How long to stay (at a glance)

[CONFIRMED] The consensus across independent itineraries and guides is that at least three days is ideal to see Belgrade's main sights plus a couple of neighbourhoods, with compressed 1- and 2-day options also common. Three days lets a visitor add some "hidden gems" beyond the centre.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.5; repeated 2021–2026 itinerary consensus; LOTUS EATERS TRAVEL; TripWis 2025)

[CONTEXT] This page anchors on the 3-day shape (the highest-intent planning query) and can fold 1-day/2-day as compressed variants. It assumes the orientation and "why" come from the spine pages; here the job is sequencing and logistics.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §A.1, §A.3)

## 2. The walkable core and how things cluster

[REPORTED] Belgrade's central sights cluster for walking: Republic Square → Knez Mihailova → Kalemegdan, with Skadarlija adjacent, form a natural first-day spine; Vračar (Temple of St. Sava, Nikola Tesla Museum) is a short hop; Zemun, Ada Ciganlija and Avala are half-day add-ons. The central core is best on foot; the wider city has heavy traffic.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3, §C.5)

[CONFIRMED] Public transport (buses, trams, trolleybuses) has been free for all passengers since 1 January 2025, so moving between clusters and out to Zemun/Ada carries no fare. (Policy current as of 2026; perishable — re-check closer to travel.)
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.4; B92 2024-12-17; AndyBTravels)

[REPORTED] The free walking-tour spine (Republic Square → Knez Mihailova → Kalemegdan → Skadarlija, ~2–2.5h, tip-based ~€10–20) is a natural Day 1 orientation; thematic tours (20th-century history, underground/"Mysterious") suit later days. Tour prices/schedules perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §A.4, §E.6)

## 3. Sites to sequence (practicals linked, not restated)

[CONFIRMED] Core stops and their broad timing/access, all detailed on their canonical pages: Belgrade Fortress (free, 24/7 grounds; interior sites seasonal/ticketed → `understanding-belgrade`); Temple of St. Sava (free, ~08:00–20:00); Nikola Tesla Museum (timed hourly guided tours, ~800 RSD, cash only → `belgrade-museums`); Republic Square + Knez Mihailova (open public space); Ada Ciganlija (free park, May–Sep facilities). Hours/prices indicative as of 2026.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3, §E.1–E.2)

[CONTEXT] A common 3-day shape (illustrative, not prescriptive): Day 1 central core + walking tour + Skadarlija evening; Day 2 Vračar (St. Sava + Tesla) + a museum + New Belgrade Brutalism half-day; Day 3 Zemun or a day trip. Present as one sensible option, not the only route.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §C.3–C.5)

## 4. Extending the trip

[REPORTED] If staying longer, the standard add-on is a day trip — Novi Sad is self-guided-friendly (high-speed train ~32–36 min or bus ~1h10, cheap); the combined Novi Sad + Sremski Karlovci + Fruška Gora monasteries trip is tour-preferred; Iron Gates/Đerdap and Oplenac/Topola are tour-dependent. Full day-trip detail and costs on `belgrade-day-trips`. Fares/prices perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.5)

## 5. Cost and timing context

[REPORTED] Rough daily budget bands as of 2026: budget ~$50/day, mid-range ~$150–170/day, comfort/luxury $250–450+/day. Belgrade is affordable but no longer ultra-cheap. (Full budget framing on `is-belgrade-worth-visiting`.) Indicative/perishable.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.4)

[CONFIRMED] Best months are April–June and September–October (mild; avoids July–August heat). Note: Expo 2027 runs 15 May–15 August 2027 and will intensify summer crowds and prices — visitors with flexible dates may prefer the shoulder months that year.
(Source: RESEARCH_visit-belgrade.md §E.4; ClimatesToTravel; expobelgrade2027.org)
